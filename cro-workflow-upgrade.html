How our CRO workflow
got smarter

A plain-English summary of what changed between our old system and the one we use today — and why the difference matters for the quality of your test ideas.

The Problem We Solved
Root Cause

In the old workflow, data collection and strategic analysis happened in the same session. By the time we were generating test ideas, the AI was carrying the full weight of intake work — reviews, ads, speed data, screenshots — all loaded at once. The result: safe, predictable recommendations shaped by data volume, not sharp thinking.

The fix wasn't to collect less data. It was to separate the jobs so each stage gets a clean slate and full focus.

What This Changes in Practice
01
Better test ideas

The ideation stage now starts with a clean context window. The AI isn't reasoning through a backlog of intake data — it's reading a pre-ranked shortlist and applying CRO pattern research at the exact moment it's useful. Ideas are more specific and more creative.

02
Smarter slot prioritization

The audit now ends with a scored opportunity list — evidence strength × revenue potential × how fixable it is. The roadmap reads this list first and picks slots top-down. Priority is derived once, correctly, not re-guessed in a different session.

03
Screenshots used at the right moment

In the old system, screenshots were referenced during collection — adding visual weight before any analysis began. Now they stay on disk and are loaded only during the audit, when the AI is actually analyzing the site experience.

04
Consistent, polished deliverables

The HTML roadmap is now a dedicated stage with its own clean session. Brand colors, typography, and layout are extracted fresh from the homepage each time — not inherited from a previous session's context.

Bottom Line

Each stage gets the full budget of intelligence for exactly the thing it's responsible for. Collection is purely mechanical. Analysis is thorough. Ideation is creative. Output is polished. None of these jobs dilute the others.
