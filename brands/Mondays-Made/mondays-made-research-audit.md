# Mondays Made CRO Research Audit

## Data Sources Used

**User-provided:** Meta Ads & Landing Pages (visual summary + screenshots), Google Ads (visual summary + screenshots), Reviews & UGC (80+ reviews, Jul 4-8 2026), PageSpeed JSON (homepage + PDP, collected Jul 8 2026), Current Site Screenshots (homepage, collection, PDP, cart).

**Self-researched:** Competitor analysis (Jul 8 2026). Live site text fetched via WebFetch for homepage and all 3 ad landing pages.

## Source Findings

### Meta Ads & Landing Pages

Three ads running as of collection date (Jul 8 2026). All running across Facebook/Instagram/Messenger/Threads as static images.

**Ad 1 — Carved Sakura Ring (running since May 26 2026)**
- Creative angle: Craftsmanship/material story. "Floral jewelry, made differently. Each piece is carved from natural gemstones — designed to feel softer, more organic... Crafted in 18k gold vermeil with 5x thicker plating."
- LP: Dedicated PDP for Carved Sakura Ring (US$129). Split-screen image, buy box with all three material claims restated, "Complete The Look" cross-sell, and a 24-review section visible by fold 3.
- Message match: Strong. Ad leads with gemstone/vermeil story; LP delivers immediately in buy box bullet points. Specific product shown in ad matches LP exactly.

**Ad 2 — Moissanite Baby Breath Necklace (running since Jun 17 2026)**
- Creative angle: Social proof. "50,000+ customers across 100 countries. See what they say."
- LP: Dedicated PDP for Baby Breath Necklace (US$149). Opens directly to product — no customer quotes, no testimonials, no review section visible above fold or in first three folds.
- Message match: **Critical gap.** The ad explicitly promises review content ("see what they say") but the LP delivers none. WebFetch confirms: "No customer reviews are displayed on this product page." The brand has 5,000+ reviews; none are surfaced on this LP.

**Ad 3 — Carved Sunflower Ring (running since May 26 2026)**
- Creative angle: Identical body copy to Ad 1. "Floral jewelry, made differently. Each piece is carved from natural gemstones..." Different product (sunflower ring vs sakura ring), same text.
- LP: Dedicated PDP for Carved Sunflower Ring (US$129). Same structure as Ad 1. 15 reviews (all five-star) visible by fold 3.
- Message match: Solid on product. No differentiation in ad copy between Ad 1 and Ad 3 — both ads promote different products with identical body copy, which risks ad fatigue and reduced CTR for returning visitors.

### Google Ads

**Ads captured:** 3+ search text ads, 20+ Shopping PLAs, 2 display/video ads, 1 local search ad.

**Key headline themes:** "Mondays Made Jewelry - 10% Off Your First Order," "Whimsical Pieces for Your Soul - Hypoallergenic & Tarnish Free," "Mondays Made Jewelry - Versatile Reversible Jewelry," "Unique Reversible Jewelry" (local ad format). Shopping PLAs show individual product names across the full catalogue.

**Primary offer:** 10% off first order + free shipping worldwide. Appears in 2 of 3 search ad variants. BNPL mentioned in one variant.

**Gaps vs. Meta:**
- The 10% first-order offer is Meta's missing conversion lever. Google leads with it; Meta ads make no mention of it.
- Google messaging leans soft/emotional ("whimsical soul," "dainty pieces," "luxurious look in accessible prices"). Meta messaging is material/craft-focused ("carved from natural gemstones," "5x thicker plating"). These are two distinct positioning angles running in parallel with no cross-channel coordination.
- "Hypoallergenic & Tarnish Free" is a Google headline with no Meta equivalent — despite hypoallergenic being a significant purchase driver for jewelry buyers with sensitive skin.
- "Ethically-sourced gemstones" appears in Google display ads; absent from all three Meta ads captured.
- Reversible jewelry is a dedicated Google ad category ("Versatile Reversible Jewelry," "Unique Reversible Jewelry" local ad). Absent from Meta ads entirely.

- **Back-in-stock notification:** One customer mentions explicitly hesitating on a purchase because of a sold-out item. Implement back-in-stock email capture on out-of-stock PDPs.

**Assessment:** Both pages are in the red on mobile. Homepage LCP at 7.1s (score: 0.05) means the primary visible content — the hero image — does not load for most users on slower connections before they bounce. A Speed Index of 22.3s and TTI of 27.3s indicate the page continues loading for nearly 30 seconds on mobile. The 620ms server response on the homepage is a root cause: the backend is slow to respond before any assets are loaded. The PDP is marginally better but still fails on LCP and TTI. CLS is near-zero on both pages — layout stability is good, which means the issue is load speed, not visual jank.

Industry benchmark for e-commerce: LCP under 2.5s (good), 2.5-4s (needs improvement), 4s+ (poor). Mondays Made is well into the "poor" range on both pages.

### Competitor Analysis

*Source: self-researched, Jul 8 2026. No user-provided competitor data was collected.*

| Brand | Price Range | Key Differentiators | Weaknesses vs. Mondays Made |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ana Luisa | USD $50–120 | Carbon-neutral, small-batch, ethical sourcing, DTC everyday minimalism | No carved gemstone work, minimal floral design, less distinctive aesthetic |
| Missoma | USD $80–200 | Nature-inspired, UK-based, textured 18k vermeil on recycled silver, trend-forward | Higher price point, less Asia-market focus, no reversible feature |
| Mejuri | USD $80–300 | DTC fine jewelry, gold vermeil, everyday wear positioning, strong loyalty community | Geometric/minimalist vs Mondays Made's floral, significantly higher AOV ceiling |

**Mondays Made's defensible advantages:** Hand-carved gemstone craftsmanship (genuine differentiation — competitors use cast/stamped processes), reversible jewelry (unique product mechanic rarely seen in this segment), and the Malala Fund giving-back story (ethical angle that resonates). Price point ($119–149 USD for rings/necklaces) sits below Missoma and Mejuri while offering comparable material quality to Ana Luisa with more distinctive design.

**Competitor gaps to exploit:** None of the three major competitors lead with a reversible jewelry proposition. Missoma leads on nature-inspiration but lacks the carved/hand-crafted angle. This is Mondays Made's most defensible and under-marketed positioning asset.

**Homepage:**
- Hero is editorial/campaign-first. Current hero promotes "The Summer Edit" (new collection) with a "SHOP NOW" CTA. No specific product, no price, no social proof above the fold. The 4.9★ / 5,000+ review signal is in the announcement bar, which most users tune out.

- "Why Choose Vermeil?" material education section appears in fold 3 (lavender background with 4 feature tiles: tarnish-resistant, safe for sensitive skin, long-lasting shine, accessible luxury). This is high-quality credentialing content placed where most mobile visitors won't reach it.
- Bestsellers carousel below the hero is the strongest conversion element on the page.

- 374 products in "Shop All Jewelry." Collection starts with a multi-line SEO copy block between the title and the product grid — delays first product card appearance.
- Filtering is comprehensive (7 facets) and the tag chips (Flower Jewelry / Moissanite / Reversible / Birthmonth / Engravable) are intent-aligned.
- LOW IN STOCK badges visible on several first-row items — urgency signal present but isolated to stock status.
- No quick-add-to-cart on hover. Each product requires a full PDP visit to add to bag.

**PDP:**
- Buy box is sticky — visible across all three captured folds on both the ring and necklace PDPs. This is the strongest structural conversion element on site.
- Star rating (5★) is shown in the buy box, but no review count and no review text visible in the first three folds on at least one PDP (Baby Breath Necklace). The Sunflower Ring PDP shows a review snippet section beginning in fold 3.
- Material annotated diagrams (fold 2) effectively visualize the multi-gemstone composition.
- Length/size guides (fold 3) are well-executed for necklaces and rings.
- No urgency signals (countdown timer, low-stock warning) in the buy box.

**Cart Drawer:**
- No product recommendations, upsells, or cross-sells.
- Free shipping progress bar shows threshold already met at $149 ("Your order total qualifies for free shipping!") — the only AOV incentive is satisfied immediately, removing all motivation to add more items.
- Large blank white space between the single line item and the subtotal.
- No gift messaging field, no loyalty point display, no second spend threshold.
- Checkout CTA is prominent (full-width purple) — conversion flow itself is clean.

## Cross-Source Themes

**Ranked by evidence strength x revenue potential x funnel importance:**

**1. Mobile speed blocks all paid traffic before conversion.**
Evidence from 2 sources (PageSpeed). Homepage LCP 7.1s and TTI 27.3s mean the majority of mobile visitors — who arrive from Meta and Google ads — experience a near-empty page for 7+ seconds. All other conversion improvements are diluted while this remains unfixed. This is the highest-leverage technical fix in the audit.

**2. Social proof is absent at the moments it most matters.**
Evidence from 3 sources (Meta Ads, Site Screenshots, WebFetch). Ad 2 explicitly promises "see what they say" but the LP shows no reviews. PDPs show only aggregate rating in the buy box with no review text visible in fold 1-3. The brand has 5,000+ verified reviews and a deeply enthusiastic customer base — this asset is almost entirely unused at the point of purchase decision.

**3. The brand's strongest differentiator (reversible jewelry) is invisible in Meta.**
Evidence from 3 sources (Google Ads, Meta Ads, Reviews). Google runs dedicated ad copy for reversible jewelry. Multiple reviewers call out the reversible feature as unique and the reason they love their purchase. Meta ads say nothing about it. Customers who convert on reversibility are coming in through Google only.

## Top Test Opportunities

Ranked by evidence strength x revenue potential x fixability.

**1. Ad 2 Social Proof LP — Inject review content above fold**
The social proof-led ad ("50,000+ customers. See what they say.") drives to a PDP with zero visible reviews. The promise made in the ad is broken on landing. Evidence: Meta Ads visual summary (explicit gap flagged), WebFetch confirmation (no reviews on page). Est. lift: 10–15% CR improvement on this ad's traffic x ad 2 spend sessions x US$149 AOV.

**2. Homepage Mobile LCP — Critical render path fix**
LCP 7.1s and TTI 27.3s on mobile (performance score 49/100). With the majority of Shopify traffic arriving on mobile from paid ads, this is a conversion tax on every session. Server response at 620ms suggests a Shopify-level issue (apps, liquid render, or CDN). Evidence: PageSpeed JSON (Jul 8 2026). Industry benchmark: each 1s LCP improvement correlates to ~3-5% conversion rate lift. Est. lift: fixing LCP from 7.1s to under 3s could recover 10–20% of sessions that bounce before the page loads.

**3. Cart AOV — Second spend threshold + cross-sells**
The cart drawer has a single line item, zero recommendations, and a free shipping bar already satisfied at $149. There is no mechanism to drive a second product. Evidence: site-visual-summary (cart section), live site WebFetch. Cross-selling "Complete The Look" items (shown on PDPs) into the cart drawer is a directly transferable pattern from what already exists on PDPs. Est. lift: adding a "frequently bought with" module + $X threshold for free charm/gift could increase AOV by $25–40.

**4. First-Order Discount Prominence — Sidebar to above-fold offer**
10% off first order exists in Google ads but is absent from Meta ads and buried in a vertical-text sidebar on-site. New visitors from Meta — the brand's primary paid channel — never encounter the offer unless they notice the sidebar. Evidence: Google Ads visual summary, site-visual-summary (fold 1 sidebar), meta-ads-visual-summary (no offer in any ad). Test: deploy a popup or homepage hero band with the 10% offer tied to email capture. Est. lift: email capture rate improvement x list LTV x first-purchase CR lift.

**5. PDP Review Snippet — Pull 2-3 quotes into buy box**
PDP buy box shows 4.9★ aggregate rating but no customer quotes. The review corpus contains multiple highly specific, emotionally resonant statements about durability, wearability, and uniqueness. These are sitting below the fold while the buy box remains unopinionated. Evidence: site-visual-summary (PDP section), reviews.md corpus. Test: insert 2-3 rotating customer quotes directly beneath the star rating in the buy box. Est. lift: 5–8% CR improvement on PDP traffic x monthly PDP sessions x AOV.

## Unused but Valuable Findings

- Ad 3 uses identical copy to Ad 1 across two different products — creative fatigue risk if both ads are reaching the same audience segments. Recommend rotating Ad 3 body copy to lead with the specific product/flower story.
- "Ethically-sourced gemstones" and the Malala Fund giving-back story appear in Google display ads and on product pages respectively, but are absent from Meta ad copy. Both are potential angle tests for a new Meta ad creative.
- The Mondays Makers Club loyalty program is referenced in the live site footer but not surfaced in any ad creative, the buy box, or the cart drawer — a retention mechanic that could reduce CAC if surfaced at first purchase.
- Multiple reviewers purchase as gifts for partners (several "my girlfriend" mentions). The gifting positioning used in Google sitelinks ("Complimentary Gifts") is not being tested in Meta.
