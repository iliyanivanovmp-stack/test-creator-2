**Star distribution:**

- 4-star: ~100 reviews (6%)

### Positive themes

- "They fade super quickly and the collar stretches out after one to two uses. I spent over $150 on these shirts and feel super bummed about it." (Nicole, Mar 2025)
- "Shirts were faded out and stretched out around the neck." (DeAngelo Burrell, Jan 2021)

- Longtime customers note a perceived shift to cheaper blank vendors (Jan Radcliffe, Jun 2020)
- Fit issue specific to women's Kerusso brand: boxy/unisex sizing means shoulder seam sits 1-1.5 inches too low when sizing up to fit the hip (K B, Jun 2021). Kerusso confirmed in their reply that classic fit tees are unisex; trimmer options exist in grace & truth and Hold Fast lines.

**Shipping cost friction (multi-year, recurring)**
- "Shipping amounts are absolutely ridiculous. Won't be ordering again." (Elizabeth, Jan 2021)
- Free shipping promotion confusion: PayPal Express checkout bypassed the free shipping discount at checkout (Roy Capati, Jul 2021)
- Two simultaneous orders, one charged shipping despite site showing free shipping promotion (loranne snyder, Aug 2020)
- Multiple reviewers cite shipping cost as a deterrent without specifying an amount

**Website UX friction**
- "Product good, website meh... not pleased when I am trying to check out and I am asked several times if I want to add another shirt to my order." (anonymous reviewer)
- "Your site is cumbersome to use." (Paula Viebrock, Sep 2020)
- Out-of-stock items not labeled in collection listings: "disappointed that so many items remained on the website, only to find that they were out of stock once clicking into the detail." (Ben, Jun 2021)
- "VERY FRUSTRATING that you have 1,000 items listed but 500+ are discontinued." (Happy Customer, Mar 2021)
- Same products available cheaper elsewhere online (Ben, Jun 2021)

**Return policy friction**
- 20% restocking fee + customer pays return shipping (Bob Younghans, Nov 2020)
- "Had to pay $7.50 and wait in a long line to return" a shirt with a lopsided print (Rebecca S., Dec 2020)
- This policy creates hesitation in a category where sizing fit is already inconsistent

**Email volume complaints**
- "Follow-up emails begging for reviews is borderline harassment." (N. Anderson, Apr 2021)
- "Could do without so many emails." (Randy John, Apr 2021)

### Client-actionable insights (not A/B tests)
- Quality control inconsistency (fading, collar stretch, shrinkage) is a multi-year problem requiring vendor/product decisions, not UX testing.
- Return policy (20% restocking fee + customer pays return shipping) creates purchase hesitation. Simplifying this would reduce sizing-related friction directly.
- Discontinue or clearly mark out-of-stock items in collection listings.

## Source 5: Competitor Insights

**Elevated Faith (primary DTC competitor)**
- Founded 2015. Targets Millennials and Gen Z with minimalist Christian apparel and jewelry.
- Price range: $35-60 (40-140% premium over Kerusso's $15-30).

## Source 7: CRO Ebook Reference (Billion Dollar Websites, Dylan Ander)

**Chapter 9: Nine Ways to Improve AOV - relevant findings:**
- Free shipping thresholds are the most common "free gift tier." They work on the same principle as unlocking perks by spending more.
- Recommended Products should sit below the ATC button to avoid scroll debt and distraction.
- One-click cart upsells: "Once The Gator starts eating, it doesn't want to stop!" The best time to ask for a second sale is immediately after the customer has decided to buy.
- Bundle-first strategy (True Classic case): brands that build their UX around bundles achieve higher AOV with minimal impact on conversion rate.

**Relevant for Kerusso:** The BOGO offer is already a bundle mechanic. The gap is that completing the BOGO ($49.98) still leaves customers $29 short of the $79 free shipping threshold. The cart shows the gap but gives no actionable path to close it.

## Source 8: CRO Community Research

**Exit-intent vs. immediate popup (Wisepops, analysis of 1 billion+ popup displays, 2024-2025):**
- Exit-intent popups convert at 3.94% on average
- Immediate popups are the #1 cause of mobile bounces
- Popups triggered after 30-50% scroll have 25% higher engagement than entrance popups
- 92% of top-performing popups appear after 4+ seconds; lowest performers appear between 0-4 seconds
- Exit-intent popups can recover 15% of abandoning visitors in ecommerce settings

**Free shipping threshold tests (ecomhint.com, Baymard Institute, cartly-pro.com, 2024-2025):**
- RIPT Apparel (graphic t-shirt brand, directly comparable to Kerusso): free shipping progress bar at $69 threshold = 7.9% increase in AOV
- Baymard Institute: 39% of shoppers abandon carts due to extra costs including shipping
- Dynamic progress indicators increase threshold conversion 15-25% vs. static messaging
- 80% of online shoppers will meet a minimum purchase threshold to avoid shipping costs
- Recommended threshold positioning: 30% above current AOV

**Page speed impact on mobile conversion:**
- Each 1-second delay in mobile page load reduces conversions by ~20% (Google, widely cited)
- A page loading in 1s converts 3x better than a page loading in 5s (Cloudflare benchmark)
- Mobile apparel CR averages 1.5-2.5%

## Cross-Source Analysis

### Theme 1: Active paid traffic is landing on a broken page
**Sources:** Meta Ads + Page Speed

Two active Meta ads (Library IDs 1393660378754994 and 1982430028973776, running since Feb 2026) route directly to the BOGO landing page. That page scores 7/100 on mobile with a 30.8s LCP. Every dollar of paid spend on those two ads is driving traffic to a page that the majority of mobile users will abandon before it loads. No other finding in this audit has a more direct, quantifiable impact on paid campaign ROI.

### Theme 2: Shipping cost is a known friction point with no UX solution
**Sources:** Reviews (multi-year) + Cart Drawer Screenshot + CRO Community Research

Multiple reviews across 2019-2025 cite shipping costs as a deterrent or reason not to return. The cart drawer (Apr 10, 2026) shows a $54.01 gap to free shipping on a $24.99 cart, with a visual progress bar but no actionable path to close the gap. The "Recommended For You" section is present but its content was not visible in the screenshot. Even customers who complete the BOGO (2 shirts = $49.98) still don't qualify for free shipping. RIPT Apparel, a directly comparable t-shirt brand, saw 7.9% AOV lift from a free shipping progress bar paired with a contextual upsell.

### Theme 3: The discount popup fires at the worst possible moment
**Sources:** Reviews + Site Screenshots + CRO Community Data

The "Get 15% off" popup fires on page load across PDPs, the homepage, collection pages, and the BOGO landing page. Reviews explicitly mention being "asked several times if I want to add another shirt to my order" as a friction point. Immediate popups are the #1 cause of mobile bounces per 1 billion popup display analysis. Switching to exit-intent would protect buying-mode visitors while capturing abandoners.

### Theme 4: No social proof on high-traffic pages
**Sources:** Site Screenshots + Competitor Research

The homepage and Best Sellers collection page have no star ratings, review counts, or customer testimonials visible. Kerusso has 1,585 Trustpilot reviews (4.3/5) and strong per-product ratings (4.0-4.4/5, 100+ reviews per product), none of which surface on the highest-traffic browsing pages. Elevated Faith leads with "80,000+ ★★★★★ Reviews" as its primary homepage trust signal.

### Theme 5: Evangelism narrative drops off after the ad click
**Sources:** Meta Ads + Site Screenshots

All 3 active Meta ads sell the same emotional benefit: "your T-shirt sparks a life-changing conversation about faith." The PDPs present a product and a price without continuing that narrative. The homepage hero (golf collection) has no connection to the active paid campaigns. Customers who arrive from ads and land on the homepage see a completely different offer.
