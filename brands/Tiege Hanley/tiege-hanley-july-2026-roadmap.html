Cross-Source Findings

Biggest Killers of Conversion Rate

  • Simplicity is promised across acquisition and validated in reviews, but the priority landing experience introduces choice before a recommended path.
  • Proof is strong but arrives late or disappears after the click. The site has 26,525 verified reviews and before-and-after assets, while entry heroes lead with discounts.
  • Value skepticism centers on quantity and efficacy. Product volumes are visible, but the 30-day usage case is not explained near purchase.

What the Data Shows

Meta Ads and Landing Pages

  • Paid-social creative emphasizes clinically proven results, visible improvement, and uncomplicated skincare. The bundle-builder destination opens with up to 40% off, a free gift, and product selection.
  • The clinical proof and time-to-result claims are absent above the landing-page fold.
  • Landing destinations consistently prioritize discounts over the creative's proof and simplicity claims.

Google Ads

  • Search, Shopping, display, and video units consistently use “uncomplicated,” “made easy,” and “skin care that works.”
  • One visible search unit shows 4.6 stars from 537 reviews.
  • No explicit discount appears in the collected Google copy, while onsite entry points lead with up to 40% off.

Reviews and UGC

  • Simplicity is the clearest repeated advantage. Customers value straightforward routines, included instructions, and AM/PM structure.
  • Perceived quantity-to-price value is the dominant objection. Reviews call the bottles “tiny,” “skimpy in size,” and “not worth the price.”
  • Product-tolerance concerns include dryness, oily AM moisturizer, eye burning, an abrasive scrub, and SPF20 being perceived as inadequate. These are individual reports, not clinical findings.

Page Speed Insights

  • The bundle builder scored 27/100 in simulated mobile Lighthouse testing, with 38.7s LCP and 3,090ms TBT.
  • The homepage scored 28/100, with 22.3s LCP and 2,360ms TBT.
  • CLS was 0 in both lab runs. Rendering and main-thread blocking were the observed problems.

Site Screenshots

  • The homepage hero leads with up to 40% off. Routine levels and the 4.7-star, 26,525-review proof arrive in the next fold.
  • The Level 1 buy box clearly presents supply, purchase options, pricing, free shipping, cancellation, guarantee, ratings, and the CTA, but does not explain how the product sizes last 30 days.
  • The cart confirms free shipping at $35. Its eye-cream cross-sell has no visible price, rationale, bundle saving, or direct add control in the captured view.

Competitor Catalogs

  • Tiege Hanley's observed $35 subscription was below the compared official routines from Geologie, Lumin, and Brickell.
  • Geologie makes personalization explicit, Lumin uses concern-led naming, and Brickell frames its offer as a full-size routine.
  • Tiege Hanley's defensible distinction is a pre-structured, beginner-friendly AM/PM system, not price alone.
