# Tiege Hanley CRO Research Brief

**Data Sources:** Meta Ads Library creatives and landing-page screenshots; Google Ads Transparency screenshots; Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026; mobile Lighthouse reports collected July 1, 2026; homepage, Level 1 PDP, and cart screenshots; official Geologie, Lumin, and Brickell storefront catalog data researched July 1, 2026

The highest-traffic Meta ads and their landing page tell different stories. Ads 1 and 2 promise “Weeks. Not Months. Clinically proven,” uncomplicated skincare, and “95%” noticeable improvement. The bundle builder opens with “Up to 40% Off Premium Skin Care,” a free gift, and no matching proof above the fold. Source: Meta Ads Library creatives and landing-page screenshots.

The page then makes a simple routine feel complex. Visitors move through six product tabs, five routine tabs, a three-column product grid, four discount tiers, and an empty “Your Routine” panel. Customers value the opposite experience: “The routine is straightforward and easy to follow. No more guessing which product to use when.” Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026 and landing-page screenshots.

Price is not the core value problem. Reviews dated 2019-2026 repeatedly describe the products as “tiny,” “skimpy in size,” and “not worth the price.” Tiege Hanley's $35 subscription is below the observed Geologie $49, Lumin $63, and Brickell $49.20 routines. The missing piece is proof that the included quantities deliver a full 30-day routine. Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026, Level 1 PDP screenshots, and competitor storefront catalog data researched July 1, 2026.

The priority landing page also carries the highest performance risk. Mobile Lighthouse testing on July 1, 2026 scored the bundle builder 27/100 with 38.7s LCP and 3,090ms TBT, compared with 28/100, 22.3s LCP, and 2,360ms TBT on the homepage. These are lab results, not field Core Web Vitals. Revenue potential cannot be calculated because monthly sessions and AOV are unknown. Source: mobile Lighthouse reports collected July 1, 2026.

## Slot 1: Match the Bundle Hero to the Ad Promise

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Bundle Builder (https://www.tiege.com/pages/bundle-builder)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x 3% CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we lead with the clinically proven, weeks-not-months promise from Meta Ads 1 and 2, more paid visitors will complete the bundle builder because the landing page will immediately confirm the reason they clicked.

**Data:** Meta Ads 1 and 2 share this destination and promise “Weeks. Not Months. Clinically proven,” uncomplicated skincare, and “95%” noticeable improvement, while the hero only promotes up to 40% off and a free gift. Source: Meta Ads Library creatives and landing-page screenshots. The homepage reports 26,525 verified reviews, but proof is absent above the bundle-builder fold. Source: homepage and landing-page screenshots.

**V1:** Replace the “Up to 40% Off Premium Skin Care” headline with the ads' “Weeks. Not Months. Clinically proven” promise and add the “95%” noticeable-improvement proof beside it. Keep the free-gift line and green “BUILD BETTER SKIN” button unchanged, with the discount offer moved to secondary copy. On mobile, keep the matched promise, proof, offer, and CTA visible in the first screen in that order. On desktop, keep the same hierarchy within the existing dark hero.

## Slot 2: Lead With a Recommended Routine

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Bundle Builder (https://www.tiege.com/pages/bundle-builder)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x 2% CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we show a complete recommended routine before customization, more visitors will add a routine because they can choose a clear starting point without navigating eleven filters and tabs.

**Data:** Six product filters and five routine tabs appear before a three-column grid, while the sticky “Your Routine” panel starts empty and shows four discount tiers. Source: landing-page screenshots. Customers praise the routine because there is “No more guessing,” and Meta Ad 1 says “KEEP IT UNCOMPLICATED.” Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026 and Meta Ads Library creatives.

**V1:** Replace the initial empty “Your Routine” state with one prebuilt routine that shows its included products, AM/PM sequence, result, price, and one add CTA. Move the existing product filters and routine tabs below it as the secondary customization path. On mobile, stack the routine contents, sequence, price, and CTA before customization. On desktop, place the complete routine in the existing sticky panel beside the product area, with customization below the recommended option.

## Slot 3: Prove the 30-Day Supply

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Level 1 PDP (https://www.tiege.com/products/skin-care-system-level-1)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x 1.5% CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we explain how each product lasts for the selected 30-day period, more visitors will click the $35 subscription CTA because the buy box will answer the repeated quantity objection at the point of purchase.

**Data:** Reviews dated 2019-2026 repeatedly call the bottles “tiny” and “not enough”; one reviewer said a “30-day supply” appeared to contain “maybe 2 weeks of product.” Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026. The buy box shows a 2.5 fl oz wash, three 0.75 fl oz products, 30-day and 90-day toggles, and a $35 subscription beside a $45 one-time option, but it does not explain doses, expected applications, or duration. Source: Level 1 PDP screenshots.

**V1:** Add one quantity-proof block directly below the 30-day and 90-day supply selector. For each included product, show the use amount and expected applications, then close with the documented duration guarantee. Keep the subscription and one-time cards and the green $35 CTA unchanged. On mobile, place the block immediately after the supply selector and before the purchase cards. On desktop, keep it within the right-side sticky buy box in the same position.

## Slot 4: Explain the Full-System Value on the Homepage

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Homepage (https://www.tiege.com/)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x unknown CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we lead with the full-system value proposition and keep the discount secondary, more offer-focused visitors will start shopping because the homepage will explain what the system includes before asking for action.

**Data:** Offer-focused creative promises a “Full system, nearly half the price,” while the homepage hero says “Start Today and Get Up To 40% Off!” without explaining the routine, product set, expected result, or proof. Source: Meta Ads Library creatives and homepage screenshots. Routine levels and the 4.7-star/26,525-review badge arrive in fold 2, while before/after evidence appears in fold 3. Source: homepage screenshots.

**V1:** Replace the discount-led homepage headline with the documented full-system value proposition and add a compact line naming the structured routine beneath it. Keep the existing green “START NOW” CTA and move “Up To 40% Off” to secondary copy. On mobile, keep the system proposition, support line, offer, and CTA visible in the first screen. On desktop, preserve the existing lifestyle-image hero and use the same hierarchy.

## Slot 5: Make the AM/PM Routine Easy to Scan

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Homepage (https://www.tiege.com/)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x unknown CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we explain the AM/PM routine and routine levels immediately after the hero, more visitors will select a system because the page will deliver the uncomplicated guidance promised in acquisition.

**Data:** Google acquisition repeatedly uses “uncomplicated,” “made easy,” and “skin care that works,” while the homepage first fold does not explain the routine or product set. Source: Google Ads Transparency screenshots and homepage screenshots. Customers praise the straightforward routine, instructions, and clear AM/PM structure. Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026.

**V1:** Add a compact routine explainer directly below the homepage hero with one AM sequence, one PM sequence, and the existing routine levels as clear starting points. Keep detailed product information farther down the page. On mobile, stack AM, PM, and routine-level choices in one vertical flow. On desktop, show AM and PM side by side with routine-level choices directly below.

## Slot 6: Bring Results Proof Closer to the PDP Buy Box

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Level 1 PDP (https://www.tiege.com/products/skin-care-system-level-1)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown sessions/mo x unknown CR lift x unknown AOV = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we make the documented results proof readable beside the purchase decision, more visitors will add the Level 1 system because they can evaluate outcomes without leaving the buy-box area.

**Data:** Results thumbnails near the purchase area are small, while fuller before/after and philosophy content appear farther down the PDP. Source: Level 1 PDP screenshots. Some reviewers question differentiation or report no major change after one month, while others report clearer, smoother, and better-hydrated skin. Source: Amazon reviews dated July 2019-June 2026.

**V1:** Replace the small results thumbnails near the buy box with one larger before/after result and a control to view the remaining documented examples. Keep the supply selector, purchase cards, guarantee, and CTA unchanged. On mobile, place the proof module immediately before the supply selector. On desktop, place it above the selector within the existing right-side purchase column.

## Slot 7: Make the Cart Cross-Sell Decision Complete

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** Cart Drawer (sitewide)
**Revenue potential:** Unknown cart sessions/mo x unknown attach-rate lift x unknown cross-sell price = not calculable.

**Hypothesis:** If we show the eye-cream price, recommendation rationale, savings context, and a direct add control, more cart visitors will add it because the drawer will provide the information needed to evaluate the recommendation.

**Data:** The captured cart reaches free shipping at $35, so the shipping threshold no longer motivates a larger order. Its single eye-cream recommendation has no visible price, recommendation rationale, bundle saving, or direct add control in the captured view. Source: cart screenshot.

**V1:** Expand the existing eye-cream recommendation into a complete cross-sell card with its documented product image, visible price, concise recommendation rationale, any verified saving, and a direct add control. Do not change the cart line item, free-shipping message, total, or checkout CTA. On mobile and desktop, keep the card inside the drawer between the line item and order total.

## Slot 8: Fix Bundle Builder Mobile Performance

**Type:** Immediate Fix
**Page:** Bundle Builder (https://www.tiege.com/pages/bundle-builder)

**Why this is the priority:** The highest-priority paid landing page scored 27/100 in simulated mobile Lighthouse testing on July 1, 2026, with 38.7s LCP and 3,090ms TBT. These delays can postpone both the offer and interactive product selection.

**What we're fixing:** Reduce unused JavaScript and main-thread work on the bundle builder, prioritizing the above-the-fold hero and the controls required to begin product selection. Preserve the current customer-facing design while removing or deferring non-critical work.

**Key requirements:**
- Validate changes against the July 1, 2026 mobile Lighthouse baseline.
- Preserve bundle selection, discount-tier, sticky-routine, and add-to-cart behavior.
- Load the hero and first interactive selection controls before below-the-fold assets.

**Success metrics:**
- Improve mobile LCP and TBT from the documented 38.7s and 3,090ms lab baselines.
- Confirm no functional regression across product selection and cart handoff.
