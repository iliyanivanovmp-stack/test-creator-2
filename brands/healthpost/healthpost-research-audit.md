## Meta Ads

3 ads analyzed. All have been active since April 22, 2026. All show "multiple versions" active. These 3 represent a sample of HealthPost's broader Meta campaigns.

### Ad 1 (Library ID: 991136776762043)
- **Creative:** Bold orange graphic. "Save $15, When you spend $75, Use code: SPEND75"
- **Copy:** "Save $15, When You Spend $75. Limit one use per customer, T&C's applies."

No mention of SPEND75, the $15 saving, or the $75 spend threshold anywhere visible above the fold. A visitor who clicked on this ad expecting a $15 discount gets no confirmation or guidance.

### Ad 3 (Library ID: 806545192139260)
- **Creative:** Product image of Weleda Arnica Sport Massage Oil

## Reviews & UGC

### SLO Cedar + Sage Natural Deodorant (29 reviews, 4.5 stars)

**What customers love:**
- Effectiveness as an aluminium-free deodorant (most common theme across reviews): "surprisingly effective," "possibly most effective natural deodorant I have tried," "certainly does the job," "surprisingly more effective than some other brands"
- Eco/zero-plastic packaging. Multiple reviewers mention it unprompted: "Love the lack of plastic!", "super stoked with the zero plastic cardboard tube!!", "impressive no-plastic packaging," "I love the cardboard packaging, great for the environment"
- Works for active/sporty users: "I do physical sweaty work too - normally have to reapply with other brands," one reviewer convinced their "sporty and sweaty partner" to try it and it worked
- Scent: "beautiful subtle and sweet, earthy and fresh," "lovely scent, long lasting," "not too flowery," "scents are amazing and natural"
- Long-lasting compared to other natural deodorants
- Very high repeat purchase intent: nearly all reviewers state "Would repurchase: Yes"
- Cross-gender appeal: several women purchasing it for male partners, who also love it

**Friction points:**
- Hard to push up near end of stick: "Initially it was hard to push up the product but I used my toothbrush handle" (Spancha, 19/06/24)
- Clothing residue: "leaves a very greasy residue on my clothing so I won't be purchasing again" (Julia B., undated)
- One strongly negative review: "hard, dry stick with hardly any fragrance that actually made my BO worse. Going to go back to using the Woohoo brand" (Jenny, 10/09/21)
- Scent fades at end of stick: "by the end of the stick the scent has pretty much all gone" (Clair., 09/12/23)

**Reviewer demographics (where provided):** Predominantly female, ages 35-65+. Several male reviewers noted in female-authored reviews (husbands, partners).

**Client-actionable (not test ideas):** Stick mechanism and clothing residue are product-level issues. Worth flagging to the brand (slo).

## CRO Ebook Reference (Billion Dollar Websites - Dylan Ander)

Relevant sections applied to this audit:

- **Review count as trust signal above fold:** "You can use badges that state things like 'More than 10,000+ customers' or include it in your headline... Here's a stellar example of a coffee brand that has over 40,000 reviews. This is an incredible signal to increase trust right above the fold." Directly supports Slot 2 (PDP trust injection).

- **Cart social proof:** "Add Social Proof. Try adding testimonials or reviews inside the Cart and Checkout process." HealthPost has a trust bar in the cart but it shows "7,800+ reviews" - inconsistent with the "65,000+" promise in ads.

- **Pre-purchase upsell risk:** "Pre-purchase upsells are 'one-click buys' in your Cart and are generally great for increasing AOV, but they can hurt your sales if done wrong. If your upsell puts users back into consideration mode, they are more likely to abandon their Cart." The Arnica Cream upsell is complementary, but not bridging the free shipping gap limits its effectiveness.

- **Discount code distraction:** The ebook advises removing non-conversion distractions from the checkout flow. The visible "Discount code +" field in the cart is a known abandonment trigger, especially when an active promo code (SPEND75) is running simultaneously on Meta.

- **Landing page vs homepage for paid traffic:** "If users hit your Landing Page and bounce immediately, there is clearly an issue with your Hero section." The SPEND75 ad creates a specific expectation that the homepage completely fails to validate.

## CRO Community Research

- Dedicated landing pages converted at nearly 3x the rate of homepages for branded paid search traffic in an Instapage A/B test. A message-matched headline test (ad headline matched to landing page headline) drove a 42% lift in conversions in two weeks.
- One A/B test reported a 9% drop in cart abandonment by optimizing discount code field placement (moving from visible to collapsed or checkout-only).
- Hiding discount code fields in cart reduces code-hunting abandonment by eliminating the cognitive prompt to leave the cart and search for a code.

Sources: Instapage (branded search LP test), Aureate Labs (cart A/B testing 2025), VWO Blog, CirklStudio.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: Paid traffic lands on pages that break the ad promise
*Sources: Meta Ads + Site Screenshots + CRO Community Research*

Ad 1 sends SPEND75 promo traffic to a homepage promoting a different sale entirely. Ads 2 and 3 promise "65,000+ reviews" but land on PDPs showing 18-29 reviews with no bridge to the sitewide count. Each ad creates an expectation; each landing page contradicts it. Research shows dedicated, message-matched landing pages convert at nearly 3x vs. homepage for paid traffic.

### Theme 2: Review count fragmentation erodes trust at every funnel stage
*Sources: Meta Ads + Google Ads + Cart Screenshot + PDP Screenshots*

65,000+ (Meta ads) - 54,000+ (Google ad) - 7,800+ 5-star (cart) - 18-29 (PDPs). A customer who sees all of these numbers in one session will notice the inconsistency. The ad uses a large count to establish trust. The product page undermines it with a number 2,000-3,600x smaller and no explanation.

### Theme 3: Mobile page speed is catastrophic for a site spending on paid ads
*Sources: Page Speed Report*

PDP LCP: 7.0s on mobile (2.8x the 2.5s threshold). Homepage LCP: 5.3s (2.1x). Every mobile click from a Meta or Google ad encounters this delay. Per Google/Deloitte research, each additional second of mobile load reduces conversions by up to 20%.

### Theme 4: The cart is designed to encourage discount-hunting
*Sources: Meta Ads + Cart Screenshot + CRO Research*

HealthPost is actively running a promo code (SPEND75) in Meta ads. The cart displays a visible "Discount code +" field. Any shopper who did not come via the SPEND75 ad will see this field and assume a code exists, leaving the cart to find it. This exit loop is entirely self-inflicted.

### Theme 5: The rewards program is a retention superpower that isn't sold to new visitors
*Sources: Reviews*

Multiple long-term customers explicitly cite the rewards program as why they choose HealthPost over cheaper competitors. The program appears in the trust bar ("YOUR HEALTHY REWARDS") but is not actively featured in the homepage hero, landing pages, or paid ad messaging for first-time visitors. This is a conversion opportunity, not just a retention tool.
