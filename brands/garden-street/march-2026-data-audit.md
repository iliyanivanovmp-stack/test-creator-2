## Previous A/B Tests

### Winners

| Test | Page | Location | Result | Revenue Impact |
|------|------|----------|--------|---------------|
| GS01 - Press Logo Bar | Homepage | Above the fold | V1 +41% RPV (desktop) | +A$9,392/mo |
| GS02 - Product Description | Product Page | Description area | V2 +21% RPV (desktop) | Deployed Nov 19 |
| GS03 - Deluxe Box | Homepage | Buy Box / CTA area | V2 +17.93% RPV | +A$41,233/mo |
| GS06 - Checkout Social Proof | Checkout | Under Pay now CTA | V1 +9.64% RPV | +A$6,145/mo |

**GS01:** Extended press logo bar with rotating editorial quotes and expanded media logos. Won on desktop, showed losses on mobile. Device-specific deployment.

**GS02:** Rewrote product description with storytelling approach ("Discover exceptional Australian gin, one bottle at a time") plus value props with icons (Exceptional value $99 vs $231 total, Welcome gift $82.50, 4.9 stars, Free shipping, Cancel anytime, Hassle-free refunds). V2 won on desktop.

**GS03:** Added individual item RRP values to "What's Inside" section + "Every 1 Month / Every 2 Months" toggle buttons (replacing dropdown). V2 with RRP values was the biggest winner at +A$41,233/mo.

**GS06:** Added "RATED 4.9 STARS FROM OVER 6,000 REVIEWS" section with 3 specific customer quotes under the Pay now button. Targeted, specific social proof beat generic trust claims.

## Competitor Research

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Price | Format | Key Differentiator |
|------------|-------|--------|-------------------|
| **Garden Street** | $99/mo | 700ml + extras | Best welcome gift ($82.50), strongest reviews (6,000+), most flexible frequency |
| **Gin Society** | $85/bi-monthly | Full bottle + magazine | Gin Journal magazine, international gins |
| **Juniper Society** | $65/mo | 500ml exclusive | First month free, every gin is a one-off exclusive |
| **Gintonica** | $49/mo | 4x50ml samplers | Live tasting sessions with distillers |
| **Liquor Loot** | $54-64/mo | 3x60ml bottles | Loyalty rewards program, tasting journal |

### Garden Street's Competitive Strengths
- Strongest social proof: 6,000+ reviews at 4.9 stars
- Best welcome gift program: $82.50 value
- Most press coverage: Herald Sun, 3AW, Delicious, etc.
- Most flexible frequency: monthly, bi-monthly, quarterly options
- Money-back guarantee (unique in the market)
- Full 700ml bottles (largest in category)
- Most complete box (gin + tonics + garnishes + snacks + recipe)

### Competitor Advantages Garden Street Lacks
- **First month free (Juniper Society):** Dramatically lowers purchase barrier for cold traffic
- **Live tasting sessions (Gintonica):** Interactive community element
- **Loyalty/rewards program (Liquor Loot):** Points for reviews, redeemable rewards
- **Editorial depth (Gin Society):** Printed Gin Journal magazine
- **Past-box archive (Juniper Society):** "Hall of Fame" creates collector psychology

### Key Takeaway
At $99/month, Garden Street is the most expensive monthly subscription. The welcome gift ($82.50) and value breakdown ($230+ total value for $99) are the strongest conversion levers against the price objection. Competitors at $49-65 have an inherent advantage with cold/social audiences.

### Landing Page Distribution

6 of 8 emails land on the subscription PDP (`/products/gin-club-subscription`). It is the most important LP in the business.

### Message Match Findings

**The subscription PDP never continues the email's narrative.** Every email tells a story (maker profiles, ingredient education, cocktail recipes, exclusivity hooks, seasonal framing) that vanishes on click. The PDP is a static, transactional page that matches none of the angles.

Specific gaps between email content and PDP:
- **Exclusivity:** Emails say "You can't buy this gin anywhere else," "Exclusive to members," "Unavailable anywhere else." PDP has no exclusivity framing.
- **Distillery stories:** Emails spend 300-400 words on maker origin stories (converted shearing shed, local mayor, Victorian High Country). PDP has zero distillery content.
- **Awards:** Emails cite "Champion Victorian Distillery 2024," "gold judges' nods." PDP has none.
- **New-member testimonials:** Email 8 features 3 testimonials from recent joiners that address conversion objections ("contemplating for ages," "kept scrolling on Instagram, finally tried it"). PDP reviews are generic and below the fold on mobile.
- **Welcome gift choice:** Email 5 details two welcome gift options (Weekender Kit OR At-Home Essentials Kit, up to $82.50). PDP shows the welcome gift but less prominently.
- **Sensory language:** Emails build anticipation ("citrus sunshine, bush honey, quince," "deep breath of fresh country air"). PDP descriptions are functional, not evocative.

### Email Content Themes

**Strongest performing themes (based on repeat use across emails):**
- Exclusivity / "you can't buy this" (Emails 2, 5, 8)
- Maker/distillery storytelling (Emails 4, 5, 6)
- New-member testimonials addressing objections (Email 8)
- Welcome gift as conversion lever (Emails 5, 7)
- "No lock-ins, skip or cancel anytime" reassurance (Emails 7, 8)

**CTA patterns:**
- Single-CTA emails (Emails 2, 4) are cleanest. Multi-CTA emails (Email 1 with 5 CTAs, Email 7 with 4) dilute the action.
- Email 1 featured 4 different products in a single email, each linking to a different destination.

### Key Insight

The emails are doing the PDP's job. Box contents, welcome gift details, "no lock-ins," member reviews, distillery stories, and exclusivity hooks all live in emails because the PDP doesn't surface them effectively. The PDP is an order form, not a sales page.
