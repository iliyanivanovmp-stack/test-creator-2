## Reviews & UGC

Reviews collected from the brand's review platform (spanning Aug 2025 - Apr 2026, mix of AU, Singapore, Malaysia, NZ, UK, US customers).

### What customers love

- **Build quality:** Consistently praised across chairs and desks. "Feels much sturdier than any other gaming chair we have bought." "Quality is solid, fabric is soft." "Great product. Comfortable, all functions practical and easily operable."
- **Licensed aesthetics:** Iron Man, Zoro, Naruto editions generate excitement. "It looks amazing." "I was so excited to open this up."
- **Comfort:** Multiple reviewers mention good back support. "I use it to work from home all day and I don't get back pains."
- **Value:** "Great quality for a good price" sentiment across multiple products.
- **Assembly:** Generally easy. "Easy to assemble. The instructions were generally clear."

### Friction points (actionable)

**1. Size/fit is the top complaint - affects AU customers specifically**
Multiple Australian reviewers bought a chair that was too small for them:
- "Not suitable for anyone above 170cm. The bottom of the headrest is pressing against my C7." (Duo V4, Singapore)
- "Back is very short. I'm not tall and headrest comes to the bottom of my neck." (Duo V4 Stormtrooper, AU)
- "I'm a 5'7 girl so this chair is too small for me. I'd have to be sitting hunched to rest my head against the headrest." (Duo V4 BunnyBliss, AU)
- "The height is a bit tall for normal tables even when already adjusted to the lowest." (Surge X, AU)
These are post-purchase discoveries. The problem is pre-purchase: customers don't have clear guidance on which chair fits their body.

**2. Shipping delay with no communication**
- "It took more than a month for the item to arrive... Timelines should be explained in advance... I am giving it 3 stars because of the delay and lack of communication." (Desk purchase - customer loves the product)
Quality product, terrible experience delivery = 3 stars. Preventable with upfront delivery timeline information.

**3. Packaging damage on arrival**
- "Some of the plastics... arrived warped, more than likely from shipping." (Maxx chair)
- "The wood for the desk came warped." (Desk)
- "It came crushed on one end... the box it was in was fine." (Guardian floorpad)
Three separate products with packaging damage complaints - may indicate a systemic packaging issue.

**4. Missing branded accessories with licensed chairs**
- "Head and back rest pillows could've been Marvel brand instead of selling separately." (Maxx Air Threads - Venom Edition)
Customers buying licensed chairs expect the accessories to match the theme. When they arrive plain-branded and sold separately, it creates disappointment. This is both a product and messaging gap.

**5. Assembly instructions quality (desks)**
- "The instruction manual is very vague. Took way longer to build than it should have." (Titus desk)

**6. Limited adjustability (ergochairs)**
- "Arm rest and backrest can't be adjusted." (AIRFLEX V2 - Indonesian customer, in Bahasa)
- "No tilt lock." (Duo V4)
- Reclining mechanism described as requiring manual holding rather than a lever lock (Duo V4 BunnyBliss)

**7. Squeaky chair after use**
- "When we rock the chair a squeaky sound comes." (Surge X)

### Client-actionable (non-CRO) insights

1. Add delivery timeline estimates to PDPs before purchase (e.g., "Ships in 3-5 business days, delivery in 7-10 days")
2. Improve packaging for desks and floorpads - multiple damage-on-arrival reports
3. Consider including themed accessories with licensed chair purchases, or messaging that licensed pillows/accessories are sold separately
4. Review Surge X chair height compatibility table (customer reports it sits too tall for normal desks)

## Page Speed / Core Web Vitals

**Report date:** Apr 28, 2026. Mobile scores only.

| Page | Performance | LCP | FCP | TBT | CLS |
|------|------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Homepage (ttracing.com.au) | 29 | 34.8s | 5.1s | 1,380ms | 0.091 |
| Easter Bundle LP (primary traffic destination) | 25 | 20.3s | 7.2s | 1,070ms | 0.17 |

**Google benchmark:** LCP under 2.5s = good. Over 4s = poor. Both pages are 8-14x over the "poor" threshold.

**Key issues flagged by PageSpeed (homepage):**
- Unused JavaScript: estimated savings of ~1,369 KB
- 110 long main-thread tasks
- Render-blocking requests

**Key issues flagged by PageSpeed (Easter bundle LP):**
- Enormous network payload: total 15,671 KB
- Avoid enormous network payloads
- 42 long main-thread tasks
- CLS 0.17 (layout shifts as page loads - failing, benchmark is under 0.1)
- Unused JavaScript: estimated savings of ~162 KB
- Unused CSS: estimated savings of ~162 KB

**Impact context:** Google research shows LCP increasing from 1s to 3s increases bounce probability by 32%. At 20-35s LCP, TTRacing is almost certainly losing the majority of mobile visitors before the page even loads. A 31% LCP improvement correlates with 8% more sales (Vodafone case study).

This is the single most urgent technical issue on the site. No CRO test will perform to its true potential while the site is this slow.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: Catastrophic mobile performance is destroying paid traffic ROI
**Sources:** PageSpeed (Homepage score 29, LCP 34.8s; Bundle LP score 25, LCP 20.3s) + Google Ads Transparency (active paid campaigns on multiple platforms) + Non-data context (brand focus is on bundle LP)

The brand's most important page - the one they pay to drive traffic to - has a 25 mobile score and takes 20+ seconds to paint its largest element on mobile. Most paid visitors bounce before they see a single bundle deal. Every CRO improvement on this page is operating at a fraction of its potential while performance is this poor.

**Priority: Pre-test prerequisite.** Raise with client immediately. No A/B test result will be reliable or scalable until LCP is under 4s.

### Theme 2: Easter bundle page has no social proof and creates decision fatigue
**Sources:** Site Screenshots (no reviews on bundle page, 7 competing options, no hierarchy) + Reviews (product quality praised) + Meta Ad 3 (drives to this page) + CRO Ebook (social proof in cart/checkout, FAQ higher on paid landing pages) + Community research (decision fatigue reduces conversion)

The brand's primary traffic destination has no reviews, no ratings, no trust signals, and no clear recommendation among 7 bundles. Customers arrive ready to buy and face a choice architecture with no guidance. Reviews clearly show TTRacing has strong product satisfaction - none of that sentiment is present on the page where purchase decisions are made.

### Theme 3: Size/fit anxiety costs conversions pre-purchase
**Sources:** Reviews (multiple AU customers bought the wrong size) + Competitor Research (Secretlab's S/M/XL system with height/weight guide is a market standard) + Site Screenshots (no size guide on PDP)

TTRacing has a quality product that multiple customers regret buying because they chose the wrong size. Secretlab - the market leader - solves this before purchase with a comprehensive size guide. TTRacing has a gap here that's directly causing both lost conversions (hesitant buyers who don't buy because they're unsure) and poor reviews (buyers who bought the wrong size).

### Theme 4: Cart drawer lacks direct checkout path
**Sources:** Site Screenshots (cart shows "VIEW CART" only, no checkout CTA) + CRO research (adding direct checkout to cart drawer = 6.8% conversion lift; express checkout in cart = 35.8% CR increase in one test) + CRO Ebook (cart is least optimized area, 70% add-to-cart abandonment)

Every buyer passes through the cart. Adding a direct "Checkout" CTA in the drawer removes a step between purchase intent and payment. Shipping cost is also hidden until checkout - showing a threshold ("Free shipping on orders over X") here could reduce cart abandonment.

### Theme 5: Message mismatch in Ad 1 is a direct revenue leak
**Sources:** Meta Ads (Swift X Ant-Man ad → Surge X PDP: different product, different price) + Google Ads (stale 2025 copy, expired promo codes)

Ad 1 has been running since November 2025 - over 5 months - driving visitors to the wrong product page. This is a quick client-action fix, not a test. Similarly, stale Google Search copy ("2025") and expired promo codes should be corrected immediately.

---

## Quick Wins (Client Actions - No Test Required)

1. **Fix Ad 1 message mismatch:** Update the Ant-Man Edition ad to link to the correct Swift X 2020 product page (or update the ad copy to match the Surge X).
2. **Update stale Google Search copy:** Remove "2025" from search ad headlines. Remove expired promo code sitelinks.
3. **Add delivery timelines to PDPs:** Prevents 3-star reviews from customers who loved the product but were blindsided by delivery time.
4. **Add "Checkout" button to cart drawer:** Low-dev, high-impact. Replace or supplement "VIEW CART" with a direct checkout CTA.
