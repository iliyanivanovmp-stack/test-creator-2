# March 2026 Testing Roadmap

**Data Sources:** Shopify Analytics, Meta Ads (Top 3), Product Reviews, Page Speed / Core Web Vitals, Competitor Research, Current Site Screenshots

## What We Found

Norse Organics does $2.99M/mo on 876K sessions at a 2.55% conversion rate. The growth is real. But the site is undermining its own best assets. You have 409 clinicians sharing your products on FrontrowMD, 6,328+ reviews, a 60-day money-back guarantee, and an Arctic botanical story no competitor can touch. None of that leads on your highest-traffic pages. Instead, visitors land on "TODAY ONLY: ACNE DAY SALE 45%," a countdown timer, "LIMITED STOCK," and "OUR BIGGEST DISCOUNT EVER." One of your own customers wrote: "I thought I had probably fallen for a scam." She bought anyway. Many don't.

Your top 3 Meta ads have been running 5+ months. They work. But the landing pages don't continue their story. Ad #1 targets parents of teens and promises 25% off. It sends traffic to a general acne page showing 45% off. Ad #3 tells a compelling ingredient education story. The landing page replaces it with a discount headline. And your dedicated teen page (87K sessions/mo) is nearly identical to the general acne page with "for teens" appended, while every competitor in this space (Blume, Evereden, Bubble) speaks directly to teens with tailored branding. The ads are doing their job. The pages aren't finishing it.

## Slot 1: Landing Page Trust & Message Match Test

**Type:** A/B test (2 variations vs. control)
**Page:** /pages/stop-acne — 258,929 sessions/mo, highest-traffic landing page
**Revenue potential:** $107,370/mo from a 0.5% CR lift

**Hypothesis:** If we replace the discount-first hero with a trust-first hero that matches the ad's messaging, more visitors will purchase because they'll feel informed rather than pressured.

**Data:** The top Meta ads target parents of teens with acne, but /pages/stop-acne leads with "TODAY ONLY: ACNE DAY SALE 45%" and stacks urgency tactics (Source: Meta Ads, Site Screenshots). Reviews show skepticism is a real barrier: "I was VERY skeptical. I had a serious case of buyer's remorse" and "I thought I had probably fallen for a scam" (Source: Landing Page Social Proof, Reviews). Meanwhile, Norse has strong trust assets underused on this page: 409 clinicians on FrontrowMD, 6,328+ reviews, 60-day money-back guarantee, and a unique Arctic botanical ingredient story (Source: PDP Screenshots, Competitor Research).

**V1: Trust-First Hero (Replace Urgency with Credibility)**
Replace the "TODAY ONLY: ACNE DAY SALE 45%" headline and urgency banner with a credibility-led hero. Lead with the Clinicians' Choice badge (409 clinicians on FrontrowMD), the 6,328+ review count, and the 60-day money-back guarantee. Keep the same product imagery. Move the discount to a secondary position below the fold, presented as a straightforward "Subscribe & Save" offer without artificial urgency. Remove the countdown timer, "LIMITED STOCK," and "OUR BIGGEST DISCOUNT EVER" text. Keep one clear CTA.
**Brief:** On mobile, the hero should show the Clinicians' Choice badge and star rating above the headline, with the money-back guarantee as a line directly below the CTA button. On desktop, the left half stays as the product image (current), the right half gets the restructured copy. The scrolling "OUR RETURN POLICY IS SIMPLE" banner stays (it's a trust signal, not urgency). Remove the "WANT UP TO 45% OFF?" sticky popup in the bottom-left corner for this variation only. The discount section below the fold should show a clean price comparison: "One-time: $XX / Subscribe: $XX (save 40%)" with no countdown or scarcity language.

**V2: Ingredient Education Hero (Match Ad #3's Angle)**
Replace the discount hero with the ingredient education story that Ad #3 uses to attract clicks. Headline: "Botanical Buffet Of Arctic Plants For Blemishes" (already on the homepage, not on this LP). Below, show 4 key ingredients with one-line benefits (tamanu oil: anti-bacterial + scar healing, calendula: soothes inflammation, sea buckthorn: omega-7 for skin regeneration, beeswax: antimicrobial humectant). These are the same ingredients listed in the Ad #3 copy. Below the ingredients, add the Clinicians' Choice badge and a single CTA. No discount in the hero. Discount appears only below the fold.
**Brief:** On mobile, the hero shows the headline, then a horizontal scrollable row of 4 ingredient cards (icon + name + one-line benefit), then the Clinicians' Choice line, then the CTA. On desktop, ingredients display as a 4-column grid between headline and CTA. Product imagery stays the same. The rest of the page below the fold remains unchanged (reviews, before/afters, media logos). Remove the countdown timer, "TODAY ONLY," and "LIMITED STOCK" for this variation. Keep the "WANT UP TO 45% OFF?" popup removed as well.

## Slot 2: PDP Value Reframe

**Type:** A/B test (2 variations vs. control)
**Page:** /products/kill-acne-redness-ritual — 83,769 direct sessions/mo, plus overflow from landing pages
**Revenue potential:** $34,747/mo from a 0.5% CR lift (conservative, excludes landing page overflow)

**Hypothesis:** If we reframe the $88 price as a value proposition rather than a discounted number, more visitors will add to cart because they'll understand what they're getting instead of questioning what they're paying.

**Data:** The PDP currently shows a countdown timer ("PRICE INCREASE IN: 00:09:55"), conflicting discount percentages (22% one-time vs 45% subscribe vs 40% in header), and "LOWEST PRICE ever" claims (Source: Site Screenshots). Reviews cite price-to-value concerns: "90 bucks for this tiny travel sized" and "for the cost I had MUCH higher hopes" (Source: Product Reviews). Competitors price their teen acne systems at $39-75 (Source: Competitor Research). The Kill Acne & Redness Ritual is actually 3 products (Night Balm, Day Balm, Face Scrub), but the PDP presents it as a single item at a single price. The Clinicians' Choice section (409 clinicians on FrontrowMD) sits below the pricing section, buried under urgency elements (Source: PDP Screenshot).

**V1: Bundle Breakdown (Address Price as a Comprehension Problem)**
Remove the countdown timer. Add a "What's Inside" breakdown directly below the product title showing the 3 products in the ritual (Acne Killer Night Balm, 6-in-1 Day Balm, Premium Face Scrub) with individual values and a "Ritual Price" total. This reframes $88 as a bundle deal rather than a single product price. Below the breakdown, add "Lasts 60-90 days" with a daily cost calculation ($88 / 60 days = $1.47/day). Keep the existing pricing toggle (one-time / subscribe) below. No other changes to the buy box.
**Brief:** On mobile, the "What's Inside" section is a compact vertical list: three rows, each showing product tin icon + product name + "worth $XX." Below the list, a summary line: "3 products worth $XX. Ritual price: $88." Then the per-day cost: "$1.47/day for clear skin." Then the existing pricing toggle and ATC button. On desktop, this displays as a horizontal 3-column layout with the summary line centered below. The countdown timer is removed. Everything else on the page stays the same.

**V2: Clinicians' Choice Above the Fold (Address Price as a Trust Problem)**
Remove the countdown timer. Move the Clinicians' Choice badge (409 clinicians on FrontrowMD) from its current position below the pricing section to directly above the pricing toggle. Add the 60-day money-back guarantee line directly above the ATC button. No changes to the pricing structure itself, no bundle breakdown, no daily cost math. The buy box order becomes: product title + review count, Clinicians' Choice badge, pricing toggle, quantity selector, guarantee line, ATC button.
**Brief:** On mobile, the buy box reorders to: (1) product title + review count, (2) Clinicians' Choice badge with "409 clinicians recommend this" line, (3) pricing toggle (one-time / subscribe, unchanged), (4) quantity selector, (5) "No Acne in 60 days or money back" guarantee line, (6) ATC button. No countdown timer. No "PRICE INCREASE IN" element. On desktop, same order in the right column. Image carousel and review slider on the left remain unchanged.

## Slot 3: Teen Landing Page Message Match

**Type:** A/B test (1 variation vs. control)
**Page:** /pages/stop-acne-teens — 87,446 sessions/mo, driven by Ad #3 (long-form storytelling targeting parents of teens)
**Revenue potential:** $36,269/mo from a 0.5% CR lift

**Hypothesis:** If the teen landing page matches the parenting angle and ingredient education from the ads driving its traffic, more parents will purchase because the page will continue the story that earned their click instead of replacing it with a generic discount.

**Data:** Ad #3 is a long-form native ad written from a mom's perspective. It tells a story about discovering Norse after years of failed teen acne treatments, names specific ingredients (tamanu oil, calendula, sea buckthorn, beeswax), and critiques "Big Skincare." But /pages/stop-acne-teens leads with "ACNE DAY SALE - SAVE 45%," the same discount-first template as the general acne page (Source: Meta Ads, Site Screenshots). The page has no parent-specific language, no teen before/afters, and no ingredient education. Reviews confirm the parent buying pattern: "Works wonders with my three teenagers," "Both my 15 year old son and I use this and love it," "Teen son has used easily" (Source: Product Reviews). Every major competitor in this space (Blume, Evereden, Bubble) has teen-specific branding and messaging. Norse does not (Source: Competitor Research).

**V1: Parent-to-Teen Landing Page**
Replace the hero section with parent-focused messaging that continues Ad #3's narrative. Lead with a headline that speaks to the parent buyer directly. Below the headline, a section addressing why conventional acne products didn't work and what makes Norse's botanical approach different. Show the 4 key ingredients from the ad copy (tamanu oil, calendula, sea buckthorn, beeswax) with one-line benefits. Replace generic before/afters with teen-specific transformation photos (if available; if not, filter existing before/afters to the youngest-looking customers). Add a "Parents Love It Too" review section pulling the parent quotes from reviews. Keep the Clinicians' Choice badge and 60-day guarantee prominent. Move the discount below the fold as a clean "Subscribe & Save" offer.
**Brief:** On mobile, the hero shows the parent-focused headline, then a short paragraph (2-3 sentences) addressing the parent buyer, then a horizontal scrollable row of 4 ingredient cards (matching the ad copy), then the Clinicians' Choice line, then CTA. Below the fold: teen before/afters (if available), parent review carousel, "Subscribe & Save" pricing section, existing media logos. Remove the countdown timer, "TODAY ONLY," "LIMITED STOCK," and the "WANT UP TO 45% OFF?" popup. On desktop, the hero uses a two-column layout: left column has the headline, parent copy, and CTA; right column shows the product image. Ingredients display as a 4-column grid below the hero. One variation only. Lower traffic page plus a clear singular problem means we reach significance faster with one strong variation than two diluted ones.

## What's Next

The three slots above target your highest-traffic pages with the clearest data-backed opportunities. Here's what we're already scoping for the months ahead:

1. **Checkout abandonment.** 51% of visitors who reach checkout abandon before paying. That's your single largest revenue leak. Once we confirm your Shopify plan supports checkout customization, we'll test trust signals and pricing clarity on the actual payment page, where the drop-off happens.

2. **Homepage hero test.** 99K sessions/mo. The current hero leads with a single review quote. Testing a hero that leads with the 500K cured claim or the Clinicians' Choice badge could improve conversion for non-ad traffic.

3. **Quiz funnel optimization.** /pages/quiz-v1 gets 16,933 sessions/mo. Quizzes typically convert higher than standard landing pages. If this quiz is underperforming, there's high-intent traffic to capture.

4. **Subscription value communication.** Loop Subscriptions drives $741.7K/mo, 25% of revenue. The subscribe toggle exists but the value prop is buried behind a coupon code (VIKING10). Making subscription the default selection with clear savings could grow this channel significantly.

5. **Cart drawer trust stack.** Adding trust signals (guarantee, clinician endorsement, savings math) between the cart total and checkout button. Worth testing once the currency fix is live and we have a clean baseline.
