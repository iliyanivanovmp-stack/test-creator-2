CRO Testing Roadmap

July 2026

Flo has strong product proof, a broad mattress range, and a reassuring purchase promise. The clearest opportunities are to preserve incoming traffic intent, make product choice easier, and remove delay from the mobile purchase path.

Testing Ideas

Eight focused opportunities across acquisition, product choice, product detail, and cart.

Slot 1A/B Test

Homepage Message Match

Page: Homepage

Offer-focused traffic, orthopedic intent, and product-proof messages all arrive on one broad homepage. The captured hero carries the discount and refund promise, but several distinctive proof points appear later or are absent from the first view.

Slot 2A/B Test

Five-Mattress Decision Cards

Page: Mattress Collection

Five large product cards span two folds, but the captured cards do not offer consistent firmness, material, sleeper-fit, cooling, or rating criteria. Reviews also show materially different experiences with firmness and back support.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Slot 3A/B Test

Faster Mobile Critical Path

Pages: Homepage and Ergo PDP

Mobile Lighthouse measured a 6.5 s homepage LCP and a 7.4 s Ergo PDP LCP. Large payloads, unused JavaScript, render-blocking assets, and animated image waste delay the buying experience despite fast server response.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Slot 4A/B Test

Persistent PDP Purchase Bar

Page: Ergo PDP

The captured buy box places price, delivery, EMI, measurement help, color, size, dimensions, thickness, and quantity before Add to Cart. The primary purchase action first appears in the second fold, and no mobile sticky state was collected.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Slot 5A/B Test

PDP Suitability Guide

Page: Ergo PDP

Ergo is preselected beside an Ortho switch and 21,984 reviews, but the captured choice lacks a compact suitability guide. Collected reviews range from significant back-pain relief to worse pain, making support expectations an important decision point.

Cross-Source Analysis

Biggest Killers of Conversion Rate

  • Paid-message specificity collapses into a generic homepage. Distinctive product and proof angles are not consistently preserved in the first view.
  • Choice confidence is weaker than the product range. Five mattress types lack consistent comparison criteria, while customer outcomes vary by firmness and support needs.
  • Slow mobile execution and dense purchase controls delay action. Mobile load milestones are slow, and the PDP purchase action follows a long configuration sequence.

What the Data Shows

Meta Ads and Landing Pages

  • All collected paid-social creative sends traffic to the homepage, so specific intent is compressed into one broad landing experience.
  • Discount, trial, warranty, delivery, sleep, and technology themes appear on the homepage. Celebrity framing, the 95% five-star statistic, natural-latex proof, and vacuum-packed delivery proof are absent from the captured hero.
  • The strongest customer proof and core purchase assurances appear later in the page rather than beside the hero action.

Google Ads

  • Search creative consistently uses discount, trial, warranty, delivery, EMI, cooling, aloe-vera, and mattress-in-a-box themes.
  • Higher-intent orthopedic, back-pain, and doctor-recommendation angles still land on the homepage, where visitors must self-navigate among five options.
  • The captured hero does not substantiate a doctor-recommendation claim.

Reviews and UGC

  • 79 of 89 collected Amazon reviews are four or five stars. Seven are one or two stars.
  • Comfort, balanced softness and firmness, support, sleep quality, value, and cooling appear in positive feedback.
  • Back-support outcomes split sharply, ranging from significant relief to no relief or worse pain.
  • Durability, delivery, return pickup, aloe scent, size fit, and product-to-ad credibility appear in negative feedback.

PageSpeed Insights

  • Mobile Lighthouse scored the homepage 48/100 with 6.5 s LCP and the Ergo PDP 49/100 with 7.4 s LCP.
  • The homepage transferred 4,591 KiB and exposed 399 KiB of image-delivery savings. Two animated GIFs accounted for most flagged image waste.
  • The PDP transferred 5,798 KiB, spent 13.3 s on main-thread work, and exposed an estimated 930 KiB of unused JavaScript.
  • Server response was fast in both runs, pointing to client payload and execution as the primary problem.

Site Screenshots

  • The homepage hero shows the refund promise, up to 40% off, ₹9-per-night framing, and one Shop Mattress action. No star rating or review count appears beside that action.
  • Collection cards show imagery, positioning copy, starting price, discount, and Shop Now, but no firmness scale, material, sleeper profile, cooling, review score, or comparison control.
  • The Ergo PDP shows 21,984 reviews, then places a long configuration sequence before Add to Cart at the top of the second fold.
  • The cart recommendation asks shoppers to choose a protector size even though the mattress configuration is already present.

Live Site

  • Free delivery within 14 days, a 100-night trial, a 10-year warranty, and mattress-in-a-box proof are documented below the option-selection section.
  • The homepage places its 10,00,000+ customer proof well below product education.
  • The live announcement date and the captured sale date did not align, making offer freshness an operational concern.

Competitor Analysis

  • Wakefit makes series comparison and review volume prominent.
  • SleepyCat combines feel, suitability, review count, setup time, and policies near selection.
  • The Sleep Company leads with proprietary technology and quantified outcome proof.
  • Flo's differentiators exist, but they are dispersed across several folds and do not translate the range into an immediate personal choice.

Collection Manifest

  • All brand traffic is directed to the homepage.
  • The mattress collection is designed as a short, two-fold choice among five products.
  • AOV, monthly sessions, baseline conversion, device mix, and funnel rates were not collected.
