## Cross-Source Analysis

### Theme 1: The Cart Experience is the Lowest-Hanging Fruit (Highest Confidence)

Sources: Shopify Analytics, Site Screenshots, Winning Tests Library, Email Campaigns

The add-to-cart popup is functionally empty. It shows the product added and two buttons. That's it. No trust signals, no upsells, no cross-sells, no shipping threshold, no order total on the checkout button. Meanwhile, 1,044 users add to cart each month (Feb 12 - Mar 14), 560 reach checkout (0.89%), and only 321 complete (0.51%). The ATC-to-checkout drop is 46%. The ATC-to-complete drop is 69%.

At $726 AOV, even modest improvements are high-impact. The email campaigns show what trust assets exist but aren't on the site: BBB A+ accreditation, 4-step Customer Success Team, 100% Water Assurance Guarantee, Google/Facebook star ratings. Case studies show cart drawer optimizations generating $32K-$389K/mo in revenue lifts. The checkout button price transparency case study alone was +$389K/mo.

### Theme 2: Mobile Navigation is a Bottleneck for a Low-Knowledge Audience (Highest Confidence)

Sources: Heatmaps, Customer Survey, Site Search, Site Screenshots, Winning Tests Library

The mobile hamburger menu is the #2 most-tapped element on the homepage (298 taps, 5.14% of all interactions, last 30 days). Visitors with 3.1/5 knowledge level, who arrive via search (61%) with no prior brand familiarity, tap the menu and find a wall of text categories. No images, no guided entry points, no featured products, no quiz or sizing tool prominence.

Site search shows 2,859 searches in 30 days, dominated by submersible well pump variants (192+ combined). The desktop mega menu for Submersible Well Pumps has 30+ text links across 4 columns. The visual mega menu case study produced +$43,544/mo. The quiz-in-menu case study produced +$101,495/mo.

### Theme 3: The Homepage Hero Loses Visitors Before They See Anything Else (High Confidence)

Sources: Heatmaps, GA4, Shopify Analytics, Customer Survey, Site Screenshots

The hero-to-fold-2 transition is where visitors leave. On mobile, 40% of visitors drop off by 15% scroll (right below the hero), and 50% are gone by 20% scroll. On desktop, the pattern is similar but slightly less severe (15% drop at the hero transition, 37% by fold 2).

The homepage generates $70K+ in revenue (Feb 12 - Mar 13, GA4), 22.84% of all key events, on 8,081 sessions. But the hero is purely promotional ("50% OFF Pump Kits") with no value proposition, no social proof, and no differentiation. The current promo changed during the 30-day window (Winter Sale to DIY Your Well Sale), but the structure remained the same: sale banner with discount percentage.

Customer survey data shows service is the #1 decision driver at 4.3/5, above price at 4.0/5. Reviews show $2K-$30K in DIY savings and named team members. None of this appears above the fold.

### Theme 4: PDP Gallery Usability is a Known Pain Point (Confirmed by Client)

Sources: Client Communication, Site Screenshots, Winning Tests Library

The client directly asked about the gallery layout: "for desktop I definitely want a new format, I just don't know what could work best for mobile, or if this is the best we can do right now." Products with 20-30+ images create an unusable thumbnail grid on desktop. No sequential browsing (no arrows), no image counter on mobile.
