## Source 1: Meta Ads (3 ads analyzed, sample of active campaigns)

All 3 ads use identical body copy across different creatives:
> "Not all nutrients are absorbed the same. Your body absorbs vitamins best when they come from real food, not synthetic isolates. That's why Beef Organs focus on bioavailability, not megadoses..."

**Ad 1** (Library ID: 1466895508422723, active since Mar 20, 2026)
- Creative: Video
- Angle: Bioavailability vs. synthetic isolates
- Landing page: /pages/tiktok

**Ad 2** (Library ID: 933712316262038, active since Mar 2, 2026)
- Creative: Static product image — "HORMONE RESET KIT, $186 → $29, Last Chance"
- Angle: Specific kit offer with urgency and steep discount
- Landing page: /pages/tiktok
- **Message match issue:** Ad promises a specific $29 kit; landing page opens with the general Beef Organs subscription narrative and different pricing. No "Hormone Reset Kit" mention at the fold.

**Ad 3** (Library ID: 697562540084929, active since Mar 30, 2026)
- Creative: UGC-style testimonial from "Emma L." + "GET 4 FREE GIFTS" offer graphic
- Angle: Social proof + gift bundle
- Landing page: /pages/tiktok
- Message match is reasonable — gifts are mentioned in the landing page offer, but not front-and-center at the fold.

**Key finding:** All 3 ads route to one generic landing page regardless of the specific offer, product, or angle shown in the ad.

## Source 2: Google Ads Transparency

**Search ads observed:**
- "Primal Queen - Formulated for Women" — "Beef Organ Superfoods Are the KEY to Treating the ROOT Cause of Modern-Day Symptoms"
- "Primal Queen - Subscribe & Save" — same body copy
- "Primal Queen - 365-Day Money Back Guarantee" — "Up to 62% Off" + rating shown as 3.8★ (289 reviews)
- "Up to 60% Off - 365-Day Money Back Guarantee" — same ROOT Cause body copy, site links: Reviews, Special Offer, Formulated For Women
- "Up to 60% Off - Real Beef Organ Superfoods" — same body copy
- "Primal Queen - Subscribe & Save Up to 62%" — rating shown as 4.6★ (4,117 reviews)
- "Primal Queen - Formulated For Women" — rating shown as 3.8★ (11,514 reviews), "Most items 365+ days"

**Display ads observed:**
- "Natural Ozempic Alternative..." — PQ7 product, aggressive weight-loss positioning
- "Real Beef Organ Superfoods — We're Creating The Future Of Women's Health By Blasting To The Past"
- Shopping ads: Goddess Bites, Primal Queen Superfood Beef, individual product tiles

**Key findings:**
1. Google search ads consistently lead with "Up to 60-62% Off" framing. The landing page shows "Get 1 Month Free." These are different value propositions — the discount framing in Google ads creates a message mismatch on the site.
2. Rating shown across ads is inconsistent: 3.8★ (289 reviews) in one ad, 3.8★ (11,514 reviews) in another, 4.6★ (4,117) in a third. If these are pulling from different sources, it creates distrust.
3. PQ7's "Natural Ozempic Alternative" display ad is an aggressive claim with regulatory risk.
4. All search ads route to primalqueen.com (homepage) rather than a dedicated search landing page.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: The paid ad landing page is the brand's biggest revenue leak

Every Meta and Google ad routes to /pages/tiktok — a page that loads the hero in 11 seconds on mobile and weighs 38.5MB. Google's research benchmarks a 0.1s LCP improvement at 8.4% ecommerce conversion lift. Going from 11s to the 2.5s benchmark represents a massive potential lift. Every dollar spent on paid media is delivering traffic to a near-unusable mobile experience.

Sources: Page Speed data, Meta Ads, Google Ads Transparency

### Theme 2: Ads make promises the landing page doesn't immediately keep

Ad 2 shows "HORMONE RESET KIT $186 → $29" — the landing page opens with the general beef organs narrative at different pricing. Google ads lead with "Up to 60-62% Off" — the page shows "Get 1 Month Free." The disconnect between ad promise and landing page delivery is one of the most documented causes of paid traffic conversion loss (DTR/message match: 30-35% CR lift when corrected).

Sources: Meta Ads (Ad 2), Google Ads Transparency, CRO community research

### Theme 3: The hero section hides the product and uses weak social proof

The hero oval shows "BECOME A PRIMAL QUEEN TODAY" — no product visible. The actual product bag appears in fold 3. The testimonial in the hero carousel is generic ("I feel more balanced, focused, and energized") and the star rating visible in the carousel appears to be 3/5. Reviews show dramatically stronger, specific testimonials with high helpfulness scores (294-1,207 helpful votes) that are not in the hero. The CRO ebook: 100% of users see the hero; if they leave without scrolling, the hero failed.

Sources: Site Screenshots, Reviews, CRO Ebook (Chapter 6)

### Theme 4: Broken capsules are silently killing retention

At least 10 reviews across star ratings cite the same defect: broken and empty capsules arriving in the bag. Loyal customers who explicitly say they love the product are not reordering because of this. "I love this product but for the reason that multiple capsules were broken when I received it, I will not spend money on it again." This is a packaging/fulfillment issue, not a CRO issue — flag to client.

Sources: Reviews (3★, 4★ reviews, multiple independent mentions)

### Theme 5: The checkout discount field is sending buyers to find promo codes

The "Add discount" field is visible in checkout. PayPal/comScore research: 27% of checkout visitors abandon to search for promo codes when the field is present. One reviewer explicitly notes the Amazon vs. site pricing difference, confirming customers actively compare prices. Hiding the field behind a link can lift checkout conversion by up to 34%.

Sources: Site Screenshots (checkout), Reviews (Jen, 234 helpful votes), CRO community research

### Theme 6: Perpetual "flash sale" has trained visitors to ignore urgency

Four simultaneous urgency elements announce "Flash Sale: GET 1 MONTH FREE" with no visible expiry. The same offer has been running since at least early March 2026 (when the first Meta ad started). Perpetual urgency is ignored urgency — and it actively erodes brand trust when visitors recognize it never ends.

Sources: Meta Ads (timestamps), Google Ads, Site Screenshots

### Theme 7: Offer framing inconsistency between ads and site

Google search ads lead with "Up to 62% Off" — a discount percentage frame. The site delivers "Get 1 Month Free" — a gift/bundle frame. These are mathematically the same or similar value but feel completely different. The mismatch creates cognitive friction for visitors arriving from Google expecting a % discount.

Sources: Google Ads Transparency, Site Screenshots, CRO community research
