CRO Testing Roadmap

August 2026

BetterWild has strong product outcomes and a distinct scientific story, but paid traffic meets a slow landing page with broken narrative continuity. The roadmap focuses first on removing technical friction, then strengthening price context, purchase confidence, and cart value.

Eight priority opportunities

Where we test

Technical fixes establish a clean baseline before landing page, product page, and cart experiments.

Landing Page Speed

Page: Ads Landing Page

The paid-traffic landing page records a 13.7s mobile LCP and 38.3s TTI. This technical failure weakens every downstream landing-page experiment and must be resolved before the page can produce a clean testing signal.

What the Data Shows

Meta Ads and Landing Pages

  • Founder-led creative promises a secret reveal, but the shared landing page has no founder reference, Cody mention, or reveal.
  • Rescue-story traffic reaches a page with no rescue or foster narrative, creating only broad allergy alignment.
  • The landing-page hero shows a Shop Now action with no price or guarantee visible.

Reviews and UGC

  • 29 of 34 Amazon reviews are five-star, and repeated outcomes include reduced scratching, healed skin, and coat regrowth.
  • Customers repeatedly describe BetterWild working after vet and over-the-counter options failed.
  • Price is a repeated objection. A smaller subset also reports complete taste rejection or frustration with the time needed to see results.
  • One customer explicitly describes continuing on subscription after seeing a clear change.

PageSpeed and Core Web Vitals

  • The paid-traffic landing page scores 41/100 on mobile, with 13.7s LCP, 4.7s FCP, 690ms TBT, and 38.3s TTI.
  • The homepage scores 45/100 on mobile, with 6.8s LCP, 3.8s FCP, 670ms TBT, and 39.5s TTI.
  • CLS remains good on both pages, while load and interactivity are the dominant performance problems.

Site Screenshots

  • The Allergy Relief buy box requires dog-count and supply selections before the $59.98 add-to-cart action appears in the second fold.
  • Subscribe and Save is pre-selected, while the $79.98 one-time purchase appears as secondary text beneath the CTA.
  • The three-pack says 90-day supply but repeats 60-day supporting copy from the two-pack.
  • The cart has no guarantee, free-shipping reinforcement, upsell, or cross-sell.

Live Site Review

  • The live homepage showed Allergy Relief from $39.99, while the collection screenshot showed from $29.99.
  • A live landing-page fetch returned the embedded product state as sold out. This remains unconfirmed by screenshots and requires a manual incognito check.

Competitor Research

  • BetterWild’s subscribe price is approximately $1.00 per chew versus $0.37 to $0.48 per chew for PetHonesty and Zesty Paws.
  • Wolf-sourced probiotics and founder-led positioning have no direct parallel in the comparison set, but neither appears in the landing-page hero.
  • The root-cause gut, immunity, and skin story is more prominent than the positioning used by the compared competitors.
