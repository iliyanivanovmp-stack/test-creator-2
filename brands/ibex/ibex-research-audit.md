## Meta Ads (3 ads analyzed, sample of active campaigns)

All 3 ads started running March 26, 2026, across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and Threads.

**Ad 1 - Last Chance Sale (Library ID: 1448412626931841)**
- Creative: Woman seated outdoors in green Ibex top, "Last Chance Gear / New Markdowns" overlay
- Copy: "Ibex merino wool doesn't go on sale often - and these particular styles won't be back. If you've been considering it, now is genuinely the right time. Final colorways, retiring styles, and prices that reflect neither. Shop the sale."
- CTA: "Shop the Last Chance Sale" / "Shop Now"
- Landing page: ibex.com/collections/sale
- Message match: Ad creates urgency around retiring styles and final colorways. Landing page hero is a generic road trip image with "SALE" text. The urgency from the ad is not reinforced on the page.

**Ad 3 - Last Chance Sale, Paradox colorways (Library ID: 1650898132708809)**
- Creative: Woman in purple/lavender Ibex Paradox leggings, "Don't Miss Out / Last Chance Sale" overlay
- Copy: Identical to Ad 1
- CTA: "Shop Paradox Last Chance Colors"
- Landing page: ibex.com/collections/sale (same as Ad 1)
- Message match: Ad specifically calls out "Paradox Last Chance Colors" but lands on the general sale page. No Paradox-specific section on arrival.

**Ad 2 - Men's Natural Boxer Brief (Library ID: 3082511085269701)**
- Creative: Product shot of black boxer briefs on white background (carousel)
- Copy: "A lot can change, but our commitment to performance, quality, and merino has only deepened. Explore the latest Ibex arrivals, crafted to meet every moment."
- CTA: "Shop Now" to Men's Natural Boxer Brief PDP
- Landing page: ibex.com/collections/mens-best-sellers/products/mens-natural-boxer-briefs-new
- Message match: Ad copy is generic brand messaging, not product-specific. Landing page is a PDP for the boxer brief - the product is immediately visible, but the ad copy doesn't prime the visitor for what they're about to see.

**Key patterns:**
- 2 of 3 ads push the Last Chance Sale with identical copy - heavy markdown focus in late March 2026
- The brand is running a dedicated sale push as the primary paid acquisition message
- No product-benefit messaging in the sale ads (no "100% merino," no performance attributes)

- "Ibex Merino Wool Apparel - Ethical Everyday Essentials"

**Key offer:** "$20 off Your First Order - Code TRYIBEX20" appears as a promotion extension on multiple search ads.

**Shopping ads active for:** Men's Natural Boxer Brief, Women's Enduro Shorts, Men's Springbok Sun Hoodie, Women's Natural Bikini, Woolies Pro Tech Neck Gaiter, Moisture-Wicking Nomad socks, Women's Essentials.

**Messaging contrast with Meta:**
- Google ads: benefit-led ("odor-resistant," "UPF 50+," "breathable," "thermoregulating"), no sale messaging
- Meta ads: urgency/markdown-led ("Last Chance," "retiring styles," "new markdowns")
- These two channels are running entirely different brand stories simultaneously

**Critical gap:** The $20 off / TRYIBEX20 promotion is featured in Google search ads but is not visible in the announcement bar on the site. A popup with this offer appears to be in place (visible in PageSpeed screenshot), but the timing and targeting of that popup is unknown. First-time Google visitors may arrive primed for a $20 discount and see no confirmation of it above the fold.

**Free shipping inconsistency:** Google search ads state "Free Shipping On Orders $99+." The announcement bar on the sale page and PDP shows "Free shipping on orders over $149, US only." Inconsistent threshold across channels.

## Cross-Source Analysis

### Theme 1: Cart trust destruction (Cart screenshot + Reviews)
The EcoCart carbon offset charge is auto-added to every cart as the first line item. It appears indistinguishable from a product. It is non-refundable and reloads after deletion. At least one verified customer returned their entire order over this. This is happening at the bottom of the funnel, right before checkout, and it is a quantifiable trust issue.

### Theme 2: Message match gap between Google ads and on-site (Google Ads + Site Screenshots)
Google search ads promise "$20 off your first order - Code TRYIBEX20" but the announcement bar shows only free shipping info. New customer traffic from Google arrives expecting a discount and sees no confirmation of it above the fold. A popup appears to carry this offer, but its timing and targeting are unknown. The inconsistency between ad promise and landing page delivery is a conversion leak for paid Google traffic.

### Theme 3: Meta ad urgency not carried through to landing page (Meta Ads + Sale Page Screenshots)
2 of 3 Meta ads drive to the sale page with emotionally charged urgency copy ("These styles won't be back. Final colorways. Retiring styles."). The sale page hero is a generic road trip landscape with just "SALE" text. The emotional momentum of the ad is lost on landing.

### Theme 4: Mobile speed catastrophically slow for paid traffic destinations (Page Speed)
Both paid traffic landing pages - the sale collection and the boxer brief PDP - score 43-44 on mobile performance. The PDP LCP is 19.2s. Meta drives mobile-heavy traffic directly to a page that takes 19 seconds to load its main content. This is a direct revenue leak.

### Theme 5: Price sensitivity + quality concerns on hero product (Reviews)
The Men's Natural Boxer Brief, which has its own dedicated Meta ad and Google Shopping campaigns, has a visible waistband quality issue across 5+ reviews spanning 2021-2025. At $55/pair, this is the primary objection. Separately, a reviewer stated they would buy three more pairs if the price were lower - a signal for bundle or multi-unit offer testing.

### Theme 6: Inconsistent free shipping threshold (Meta Ads + Google Ads + Site)
Google ads say "$99+." The homepage was seen with "$99+." Sale page and PDP announcement bars show "$149." This inconsistency may confuse customers comparing purchase thresholds across different pages.
