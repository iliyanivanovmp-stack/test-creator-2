## 3. Reviews & UGC

**Sources:** User-provided reviews (Trustpilot/similar platform), PissedConsumer (2.1/5, 63 reviews as of April 2026), ComplaintsBoard (1.4/5, 70+ complaints as of April 2026), BBB complaint data.

### What Customers Love

**Intimates and lingerie (highest praise, most emotional responses):**
- "I have never had so many different colors and styles! When I put them on ohhhhhhhhh the joy I feel is overwhelming. The panties are exquisite because there is no sagging anywhereeeeeeee." (May 2022, 5 stars)
- Multiple reviewers specifically praise the intimates selection as the primary reason they shop Ashley Stewart.
- Lingerie gets dedicated ad copy investment, consistent with it being a revenue driver.

**In-store staff:**
- Multiple glowing named-employee reviews across Fayetteville (GA), Bay Plaza Bronx, North Oaks, Brooklyn, Catonsville, Memphis, Philadelphia locations.
- In-store experience is a genuine brand strength, particularly fit consultations.

**Historical quality and selection (pre-ownership change):**
- "I came across AS on FB. Saw a camo skirt with a turtle neck sweater and fell in love. Bought the outfit immediately. Since then, I've bought approximately a dozen full outfits. Everything fits great and is true to size." (Apr 2022, 5 stars)
- "I still have a lot of items from early 2000 in good shape." (Jul 2024, 4 stars)
- Core customers are deeply loyal: "Ashley Stewart will always have a supporter in me." (2017)

**Sales and pricing:**
- Customers routinely find 30-50% off and appreciate it. "Prices to me are very reasonable. Im always finding coupon codes or they are running sales." (Apr 2022, 5 stars)
- AfterPay appreciated as a payment option.

### What Customers Complain About

**1. Quality decline post-ownership change (most common recent theme, 2024-2026):**
- "Before Ashley Stewart was purchased by another company I shopped online quite a bit. They had some really beautiful clothes... This new line of clothing that they have I don't like it." (Mar 2026, 1 star)
- "I used to shop at Ashley Stewart for years. Their clothes used to be SHOW STOPPING! Now the selection is frumpy and of poor quality." (Mar 2026, 1 star)
- "BETTER QUALITY MATERIALS AND STYLES. I was a customer who would drop $350.00 in-store or online. You need a NEW BUYER, THE STYLES ARE DISTASTEFUL AND THE MATERIALS ARE CHEAP." (2026, 2 stars)
- "Did they change their manufacturer? Their clothes use to be beautiful. Now they look cheap and ugly. Always having a sale and I can never find anything to buy." (Sep 2024, 2 stars)
- PissedConsumer: "I used to love Ashley Stewart. These clothes are awful, the cheapest material I ever seen."

**2. Return policy as a purchase barrier (multiple reviewers citing this pre-purchase):**
- "$10 return label + $9.95 shipping = double charge for returning an item. They have never done that. I have been shopping with Ashley Stewart online for over 5 years." (Mar 2026, 1 star)
- "Nice selection, but their return policy is not worth the risk." (Jul 2024, 2 stars)
- "They don't include return labels anymore which is unfortunate. You have to pay to ship items back and it takes forever to receive a refund." (Apr 2024, 4 stars)
- "Order two shirts, one is too small one is too big. Want to send them back, was told one is not returnable in the store... you have to pay $10 to send it back. Ridiculous." (Sep 2025, 1 star)
- This complaint is appearing as a reason customers decide not to order, not just post-purchase frustration. It is a conversion barrier.

**3. Delivery failures with third-party carriers (escalating, 2025-2026):**
- Veho delivery service: multiple reviews citing packages delivered to wrong addresses, packages stolen, instructions not followed, and Ashley Stewart refusing to issue replacements.
- "This was the third time back to back there was an issue with Veho." (Apr 2026, 1 star)
- DHL issues also cited.
- Expedited shipping misrepresented: "2-3 business days meant preparing for shipping, not delivery time." (Feb 2026, 1 star)
- Ashley Stewart refusing refunds or replacements when carrier is at fault is a recurring complaint.

**4. Customer service breakdown (2025-2026):**
- Representatives hanging up on customers mid-complaint (multiple reports).
- No supervisors available.
- Complaints go unanswered for months.
- 30-minute cancellation window, business hours only, not communicated clearly.
- Comenity Bank credit card billing errors: late fees applied to paid balances, no resolution offered.

**5. Bra durability (specific product issue):**
- "The wire keeps coming out of the bras less than 6 months. I am a heavy chested young lady." (Sep 2025, 3 stars)
- "The bras always break down in a month or two tops. I mean they are only 30-40 bucks, but it is disheartening." (Dec 2023, 3 stars)
- Corporate does not respond to these complaints.
- Particularly damaging: intimates is the brand's most-loved category, and the most common product complaint is in the same category.

**6. Sizing inconsistency:**
- "I got a pair of Legendary Boot Cut jeans in size 16 Reg that fit so well. But then I got a pair of Fearless Skinny jeans in size 18 and I can't get into them, they can't get past my thighs." (Aug 2024, 2 stars)
- "I have to buy a 16 or 18 because of the stupid cut. Im very disappointed." (size 12-14 normally, 3 stars)
- 81% of consumers feel more confident purchasing when sizing doubts are resolved (industry data, Immerss, 2025).

**7. Style direction concerns:**
- "I would like to see more classy pieces. The prints, colors, super size legs and sleeves make us look like clowns." (Apr 2024, 3 stars)
- "Stop putting all that Garanimal looking product in the store." (multiple)
- Long-term customers want classic, A-line, sophisticated pieces. Recent direction seen as too print-heavy and generic.

**Client-actionable insights (not test ideas):**
- Switch delivery from Veho to FedEx, USPS, or UPS
- Restructure return policy (drop double-charge model)
- Address bra wire durability at manufacturing level
- Respond to corporate complaints within a defined SLA
- Reevaluate style direction with input from long-term customers

## 8. CRO Ebook Reference

Source: "Billion Dollar Websites" by Dylan Ander

**On landing pages:**
> "If paid advertising is a major source of new customers for your brand, then Landing Pages are your number one priority for split tests. Even minor improvements to high-traffic Landing Pages can have a major impact on your bottom line."
> "To test multiple changes on your Landing Page at once, duplicate your ad set in Google or Meta for each Landing Page variation."

**On cart abandonment:**
> "On average, 70 percent of people who add a product to their Cart never make a purchase. It's the biggest customer dropoff point on every Ecommerce website."
> Testing ideas: Add Social Proof (testimonials/reviews in cart), Remove the Risk (money-back guarantees, payment safety badges), Offer Free Shipping or Other Gifts, Remove Distractions (remove any links not directly related to the sale).

**On AOV / free shipping thresholds:**
> "The most common 'gift' that brands offer is free shipping. This isn't exactly a gift, but it still works on the same principle... [tools like PrettyDamnQuick or ShipHero] allow you to test free-shipping thresholds."

**On pre-purchase upsells:**
> "Pre-purchase upsells are 'one-click buys' in your Cart and are generally great for increasing AOV, but they can hurt your sales if done wrong. If your upsell puts users back into 'consideration' mode, they are more likely to abandon their Cart... One-click upsells need to be true 'impulse' purchases."

**On collection page optimization:**
> "A stupidly simple way to optimize Collections Pages is by ordering your product tiles based on their Revenue Per Session."

## 9. CRO Community Research

**On promo code fields in cart:**
- US consumers express frustration with promo codes not being applied correctly (Bizrate Insights/Digital Commerce 360, Oct 2023). A visible promo code field or link in cart actively signals to shoppers that they may be missing a discount, triggering abandonment to go find one.
- Industry average cart abandonment hit record 77% in 2025 (UPCounting, 2025).

**On sizing and fit guides in fashion ecommerce:**
- A footwear brand saw a 21% reduction in size-related returns and a 10% improvement in checkout completion after deploying a fit-prediction tool across all product pages (Immerss, 2025).
- 81% of consumers feel more confident purchasing when sizing doubts are resolved (Immerss, 2025).
- Sizing and fit information should be positioned before imagery on PDPs, including model height/size worn and whether items run true to size (ConvertCart, 2025).

**On star ratings on collection page tiles:**
- Displaying customer photos alongside star ratings lifted conversion by 7% for a UK-based fast-fashion brand (industry research, 2024).
- Brands introducing one-tap checkout options reduced abandonment by 12-18% in split tests.

## 10. Cross-Source Analysis

### Converging Themes

**Theme 1: Message match failure on paid ads (High Confidence)**
Meta Ads + Google Ads + Site Screenshots + Page Speed
- Ads 1 and 3 (beauty/accessories products, running 5+ months) send traffic to a homepage selling dresses.
- The variant URL used as the ad destination is 3x slower than the homepage (TBT 6,140ms vs 1,940ms; Speed Index 10.4s vs 6.8s; payload 6,512 KB vs 2,619 KB).
- Brand is aggressively investing in Google Ads across multiple formats.
- The Bridal ad (dedicated landing page) shows they know how to do this right. They just haven't applied it to product-specific ads.

**Theme 2: Cart friction kills buyers who already decided to purchase (High Confidence)**
Site Screenshots + Reviews + CRO Ebook + CRO Community Research
- "Enter your promo code at checkout" pink link in cart sends buyers off-page to search for codes.
- SavedBy package protection pre-opted-in, with opt-out as the secondary action.
- No social proof (reviews) in cart.
- No free shipping threshold bar.
- Credit card pitch competes with checkout CTA.
- Reviews show return policy anxiety is a pre-purchase barrier. Adding trust signals to cart directly addresses this.
- Industry: 77% cart abandonment rate (2025). 70% of add-to-cart visitors never purchase (CRO Ebook).

**Theme 3: No social proof in the purchase path (High Confidence)**
Site Screenshots + Google Ads + Reviews + Competitor Research
- 4.4-4.5 star ratings shown in Google Ads (from 9,575 to 13,388 reviews across different ads). These reviews exist but are not surfaced on the site where they would drive conversion.
- No star ratings on collection page tiles.
- No trust signals in homepage first 3 folds.
- No reviews in cart.
- Lane Bryant and Torrid both surface social proof more aggressively.
- Displaying ratings on tiles: 7% conversion lift documented for fashion brand (CRO research, 2024).

**Theme 4: Return policy perceived as a purchase risk (Medium-High Confidence)**
Reviews + Site Screenshots
- Double shipping charge ($10 return label + $9.95 shipping fee) appears in multiple reviews as a stated reason to avoid ordering online.
- This is a pre-purchase concern, not just post-purchase frustration: several reviewers cite it as a reason they are cautious about ordering.
- No return policy confidence language visible in cart or collection page (not confirmed on PDP without screenshot).

**Theme 5: Heavy discounting undermining brand value (Medium Confidence)**
Site Screenshots + Reviews + Google Ads
- Almost every product visible shows 50% off. Announcement bars cycle through additional discount offers.
- Quality perception complaints overlap with discounting: customers see low prices and expect low quality.
- Google Ads sitelinks prominently feature "Plus Size Clearance" and "Clearance Dresses," further reinforcing the discount brand signal.
