CRO Testing Roadmap

July 2026

Sharland England has strong product credibility, editorial authority, and customer advocacy, but those signals disappear across key browsing and checkout moments. The clearest opportunity is to reduce friction around trust, shipping, and paid-traffic message continuity while site performance is addressed in parallel.

Testing Ideas

Eight focused opportunities across collection, product, homepage, and cart experiences.

Slot 1A/B Test

Collection Popup Timing

Outdoor Collection and US Collection

The newsletter popup appears on arrival and covers a product card before visitors interact with the collection. Its 10% offer also repeats the announcement bar, creating interruption before product discovery.

Slot 2A/B Test

US Collection Currency

US Collection

Offer-focused US traffic lands on a collection that displays GBP prices, including £185, £395, and £1,075. The page also omits the tariff reassurance presented before the visit, leaving a comprehension and trust gap.

Slot 3A/B Test

Hadley Tray Shipping Clarity

Hadley Tray

The buy box shows £185.00, 23 five-star reviews, product selectors, and a global-shipping icon, but no delivery timeline or returns window. US reviews document shipping concern before purchase even when delivery later exceeded expectations.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Slot 4A/B Test

Cart Shipping and Returns

Cart

The cart states that shipping is calculated at checkout and provides no returns promise. This leaves shipping cost unresolved at the highest-intent stage, while the only documented one-star review concerns returns communication.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Slot 5A/B Test

Collection Card Reviews

All Collection Pages

Collection cards show imagery, names, prices, and colour swatches without ratings or review counts. The Hadley Tray has 23 five-star reviews on its product page, but none of that proof appears while shoppers browse.

Slot 6A/B Test

Homepage Trust Block

Homepage

Slot 7A/B Test

Cart Shipping Threshold

Cart

The cart offers a passive “You May Also Like” section but no threshold, bundle, or volume incentive. Existing accessories priced from £55 to £95 already appear as complementary products on the Hadley Tray page.

Slot 8A/B Test

Outdoor Collection Message Match

Outdoor Collection

Offer-focused paid traffic arrives with a promise of antique-inspired Juliette pieces, but the landing page opens on a general outdoor collection. Juliette products are mixed into the wider grid without the collection name or “timeless charm” message.

* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

Biggest Killers of Conversion Rate

  • Trust is concentrated on the Hadley Tray product page. The homepage has no reviews, collection cards have no review counts, and the cart has no trust copy.
  • Shipping remains unresolved before purchase. US customers describe pre-purchase concern, the buy box has no timeline, and cart shipping cost stays unknown until checkout.
  • Mobile page performance blocks product discovery. Homepage LCP is 27.3 seconds and Hadley Tray LCP is 29.0 seconds, with time to interactive reaching 30.9 and 33.5 seconds.

What the Data Shows

Meta Ads and Landing Pages

  • Paid creative built around the Juliette collection lands on a general outdoor page where Juliette products are mixed with unrelated pieces and the collection story disappears.
  • US-focused creative promises faster shipping and no tariffs, while the landing page displays GBP prices and does not repeat the tariff reassurance.
  • Collection landings open with a newsletter popup over the product grid. The Hadley Tray product landing avoids that interruption and has stronger message continuity.

Google Ads

  • Search advertising makes the 10% first-order offer more prominent than paid social.
  • Shopping ads use GBP pricing with both lifestyle and product-on-white imagery.
  • A competitor display ad appeared beside Sharland England search activity, showing that brand-search attention is not fully protected.

Reviews and UGC

  • Customers remember service staff by name, including Quinn, Katherine, and Celia.
  • Packaging and personal notes recur in positive reviews, including praise for meticulous packing and Louise’s thank-you card.
  • US customers praise shipping speed but also describe concern about international logistics before ordering.
  • The documented one-star review concerns a returned item with no acknowledgement and no phone number for support.

Page Speed Insights

  • Mobile LCP measured 27.3 seconds on the homepage and 29.0 seconds on the Hadley Tray product page.
  • Time to interactive measured 30.9 seconds on the homepage and 33.5 seconds on the product page.
  • The product page recorded zero layout shift, while the homepage measured 0.124.

Site Screenshots

  • The homepage leads with editorial imagery and later displays press logos from Vogue, The Telegraph, The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, House & Garden, AD, Veranda, and Interiors.
  • Collection cards show images, product names, GBP prices, and colour swatches without review counts.
  • The Hadley Tray buy box shows £185.00, 23 reviews, Berry selected, a full-width add-to-cart button, and a collapsed Shipping & Returns section.
  • The cart shows £370.00 for two Hadley Trays, states that shipping is calculated at checkout, and includes passive recommendations without a threshold or bundle.

Live Site

  • The live site includes a 4.7 Trustpilot reference.
  • The product range spans tabletop, linens, vases, ceramics, accessories, and furniture.

Competitor Research

  • Sharland England sits between accessible Birdie Fortescue and ultra-premium Soane Britain in market position.
  • The Hadley Tray at £185 is priced three to five times above the comparable Birdie Fortescue rattan tray range of £38 to £54.
  • The premium depends on design authority, provenance, and trust, while those signals are concentrated mainly on the product page.
