3 Tests Homepage, PDP, Cart Trust + Storytelling
What We Found

Weston's products earn exceptional reviews, but the site undermines that trust with inconsistent messaging and discount-only storytelling. The warranty claim changes on every page. "Free shipping" appears in trust bars, but the cart reveals a ~$200 threshold.

Meanwhile, reviews surface a powerful purchase trigger the site ignores entirely: at least six glove liner buyers name Raynaud's disease as their reason for purchasing, and none of that language appears on the homepage or PDP. The opportunity is to let the product and its customers do the selling.

This Month
01
A/B Test Homepage

Homepage Hero

Two of three top-performing Meta ads land on the homepage, where the hero is pure discount messaging with no product story or customer proof above the fold. Reviews surface powerful purchase triggers like Raynaud's relief and extreme-weather use cases, while press coverage from Good Housekeeping, People, and Men's Health sits buried below the fold.

02
A/B Test Product page

Glove Liners PDP

Six reviewers explicitly name Raynaud's disease as their purchase trigger, and multiple others highlight touch screen compatibility as the key differentiator over competitors. The current PDP bullets are feature-led and generic, missing the specific language that drives purchases.

03
A/B Test Cart drawer (site-wide)

Cart Drawer

The cart drawer shows no savings summary despite products being 25-53% off. "Free shipping" appears in trust bars across the site, but the cart reveals a ~$200 threshold. Below the checkout button, "Discounts, Taxes are calculated at checkout" creates price uncertainty at the final step.
