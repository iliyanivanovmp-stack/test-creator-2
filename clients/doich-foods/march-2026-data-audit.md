Site claims "Thousands of verified 5-star reviews" and "Loved by 5,240+ happy customers" but only 3 testimonials are displayed on the homepage. No on-page review widget with sortable/filterable reviews was visible.

### Amazon Reviews (compiled from multiple listings)

**Positive themes:**
- Genuinely good taste for a healthy product. Multiple "best healthy cookie dough" reactions.
- Allergen-friendly positioning resonates strongly (allergies, gluten-free, vegan, kosher).
- Raw + baked versatility is a selling point.
- Repeat purchase intent: one buyer got five 3-packs.
- Weight management use case: "saved my diet," "curb my need for something sweet and protein."
- Kid-approved: works in lunchboxes, toddler-safe.
- Versatile: goes with yogurt parfaits, on bananas, in freezer.

**Negative themes:**
- Price is the top complaint. One reviewer broke down $1.25/oz vs $0.18/oz for traditional brands. "Out-F'n-Rageous!" even from a satisfied customer.
- Does not match "cookie dough" expectations. Baked texture crumbles, doesn't flatten, chips are too small.
- Labeling/transparency gaps: No best-by date on container, no "refrigerate after opening" on jar, serving count ("16 cookies") feels misleading.
- Baking instructions unclear and too small to read on the jar.
- Comes melted (shipping quality issue).
- Fulfillment errors: one customer received 1 of 3 jars.
- Slow shipping: 12 days from purchase, even on Amazon.

### Reddit Thread (r/snackexchange, ~30 comments, last 6 months)

**Negative themes (majority):**
- Price objection. "$15 for one jar?" Multiple comments.
- Taste polarization. "Not cookie dough." "Play-Doh" smell/taste mentioned multiple times. "Consistency and flavor of dirt."
- Fulfillment/shipping. Orders not shipping for weeks. Lost truck of orders. No communication. Multiple people reporting "unfulfilled" status.
- Customer service. "Customer service even worse." Arrogant responses to complaints.
- Baking doesn't work. "Lost all its flavor" when baked. Doesn't flatten.
- AI-generated ads. User spotted AI-generated Snapchat ad with gibberish text on jars.
- Hygiene concern. "Finger prints mashed down into it."
- Container size change from 12oz to 6oz. Some see it as less value.

**Positive themes:**
- Parents love it for kids (no sugar guilt, allergen-free, portable).
- New smaller jar size works for on-the-go (diaper bag, purse, office backpack).
- Basic B#tch flavor gets specific praise (toffee chunks).
- Genuine repeat buyers exist: "I just housed a container."
- When expectations are set correctly ("healthy snack" not "cookie dough"), satisfaction is high.
- "Only dessert/snack I buy anymore."

**Critical insight from reviews:** Satisfied customers frame it as "protein dough" or "healthy snack," not "cookie dough." The brand's positioning creates an expectation gap. People expecting traditional cookie dough are disappointed. People who see it as a guilt-free protein snack love it.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### 1. AOV Problem: Single-First Default (HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Shopify Analytics (AOV $19.88), PDP screenshots (defaults to Single $7.99 on mobile), Homepage (anchors to $7.99 in flavor carousel), Device data (90.8% mobile)

The PDP defaults to Single ($7.99) on mobile, which is where 90.8% of traffic lands. 60% of all sessions go directly to the PDP. The homepage flavor carousel also shows $7.99 per flavor. The announcement bar promotes "Buy 3, Get 1 Free" bundles, but the page contradicts this by pre-selecting Single. AOV of $19.88 suggests many customers buy 1-3 singles rather than committing to a bundle. Meanwhile, the free shipping threshold ($45) is unreachable with a single jar and barely reachable with a 6-pack ($33.99).

### 2. Subscription is Invisible and Underperforming (HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Shopify Analytics (subscription revenue $2K / 1.8% of sales), PDP screenshots (subscription toggle below the fold), Cart drawer (subscription shows as text line item only), CRO research (subscription-led CTAs underperform vs. Add to Cart, but visibility improvements drive +18% subscription RPV)

Subscription revenue is negligible. The subscription toggle is buried below the fold on PDP. The cart drawer shows "30 day subscription with 10% discount" as a text line but does nothing to sell the benefits. Only 10% discount may not be compelling enough vs. the bundle discounts already offered (up to 37.5% on 8-pack). Research suggests elevating subscription visibility (not removing one-time) drives the best results.

### 3. Trust Deficit at Key Decision Points (HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Surveys ("sketchy" nutrition label), Reddit (scam concerns, fulfillment delays), Amazon reviews (missing best-by date, misleading serving count), Cart drawer screenshots (zero trust signals), PDP ("Thousands of verified 5-star reviews" but no actual review widget visible in fold 1)

Trust signals are thin where they matter most. The PDP claims "Thousands of verified 5-star reviews" but doesn't show an actual review count or widget in the first fold. The cart drawer has zero trust signals at the moment of purchase decision. Customers on Reddit actively check for scam indicators before buying. The nutrition label complaint from surveys and the labeling issues from Amazon reviews compound the perception that the brand isn't fully transparent.

### 4. Expectation Mismatch: "Cookie Dough" vs. Reality (MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Reddit (multiple "not cookie dough" comments, "Play-Doh" mentions), Amazon reviews (crumbles when baked, doesn't flatten, chips too small), Google Ads ("Cookie Dough, Reimagined" headline)

The product is called "Snacking Dough" on the site but is compared to "cookie dough" in ads and by customers. People who expect traditional cookie dough are consistently disappointed. People who frame it as a "protein snack" or "healthy alternative" love it. This isn't purely a CRO issue, but the PDP can set expectations better. Reviews data shows that satisfied customers cite "healthy snack" benefits, not cookie dough similarity.

### 5. Message Match Gaps Across Channels (HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Meta ads (all "Buy 3, Get 1 Free" leading to Single-default PDP), Google ads (driving to homepage, "Cookie Dough, Reimagined" absent from site, "breakfast cookie" absent from site), PDP screenshots

Every Meta ad leads with "Buy 3, Get 1 Free" but the PDP defaults to Single. One ad creative shows "Triple chocolate" but PDP shows Chocolate Chip. "DAMN GOOD HEALTHY SNACKING DOUGH" and "3X More Protein" are prominent in ads but not in PDP fold 1. Google search ads drive to homepage rather than PDP. Multiple headline angles from Google ads ("Cookie Dough, Reimagined," breakfast cookie positioning) don't appear on the site at all.

### 6. Cart Drawer Has No Conversion Support (MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE)

Sources: Cart drawer screenshots (both devices), Shopify funnel (5.62% reached checkout but only 2.31% purchased, 59% drop-off)

The cart drawer is bare. No trust signals, no free shipping progress bar, no upsell, no satisfaction guarantee. On mobile, the "Earn Rewards" widget overlaps the checkout button. The 59% checkout-to-purchase drop-off suggests friction between cart and completed purchase. While some of this drop-off happens at Shopify checkout (which we can't modify much), the cart drawer is the last owned touchpoint before checkout and it does nothing to build confidence or increase order value.

### 7. "Earn Rewards" Widget UX Conflict (MEDIUM CONFIDENCE)

Sources: PDP mobile screenshot (overlaps price), Cart drawer mobile screenshot (overlaps checkout button), Homepage mobile (overlaps content)

The "Earn Rewards" floating widget consistently overlaps critical conversion elements on mobile: the price on PDP, the checkout button in cart drawer, and content on homepage. With 90.8% mobile traffic, this widget is actively hurting the experience at key conversion moments.
