## Heatmaps (Homepage, Collection, PDP)

**Homepage (28.7K sessions, 44.2K pageviews):**
- Scrollmap: Strong engagement through the hero (IWD 20% OFF promo) and 4 category tiles ("Live On The Bright Side"). Drop-off begins at the customer review testimonial section.
- Heatmap: Heaviest clicks on hero promo CTA, category tiles, and Shop Top Picks product images. Birthday Bagheera and St. Patrick's Day sections get CTA clicks from smaller audience.

**Collection page (pajamas):**
- Scrollmap: Steep drop-off. Most users see only the first 2-3 rows of products (8-12 items). Content section at the bottom is virtually unseen.
- Heatmap: Click activity heavily concentrated on first 2-3 product rows. Filter/sort area gets clicks. Lower rows get sparse engagement.

**PDP (Courtly Creak Bagheera, Eco Satin):**
- Scrollmap: Strong engagement with product images, price, size selector, ATC button. "You May Also Like" appears twice. Reviews are deep in purple/pink territory (very few users scroll to them).
- Heatmap: Hottest clicks on product images, price, size selector, ATC. Accordion sections (Description, Details, Shipping & Returns) get clicks. Review photos get some interaction but review text gets minimal engagement.

**Key finding:** Reviews contain sizing and fabric info that addresses the #1 and #2 purchase objections (price/value and sizing), but almost nobody scrolls far enough to see them on PDP.

## Customer Surveys (214 responses, Feb 6 - Mar 8, 2026)

**Question:** "Did anything almost stop you from purchasing today?"

**Top objection themes:**

| Theme | Frequency | Key Quotes |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| Price/cost | ~55% | "The price good gravy," "would not have purchased without a sale/discount," "even then, I would buy more if the products were not so expensive" |
| Shipping costs | ~8% | "Having to pay for shipping on a $150 product," "Many companies have free shipping when you spend over $100" |
| Sizing uncertainty | ~7% | "Lack of garment measurements... armpit to armpit, widest part of hips," "didn't know if mini robe would be long enough, no length on size chart," "unclear on new sizing" |
| Stock/size availability | ~6% | "Wanted the satin one not available in right size," "not available in Tall" |
| Discount/coupon issues | ~5% | "$20 off did not work," "Didn't receive discount code after signing up" |
| Website bugs | ~3% | "checkout button failed to load on mobile and desktop," "website was glitchy" |

**New signals vs. March:**
- "I would like to be able to buy separate pieces" and "hoping to buy just the pants" (separate pieces demand)
- "fabric to wrinkle annoys me at this price point" (value-for-price friction)
- "Sewn pockets with monogram" mentioned twice (monogram UX issue)
- "came here for the bee nightgown I saw in her vlog" (influencer-driven purchase)
- "The feature asking what size you normally wear isn't very helpful when I could look at the measurement chart instead" (size tool criticism)
- "BRING BACK YOUR CAFTANS!" (product demand, confirmed by zero-result search for "kaftan")

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

**Theme A: Conversion Rate Decline on Full-Price Traffic (5 sources)**
Shopify (CR 1.04%, down from 1.61%), Intelligems (weekly CVR declining: 1.13% to 0.88%), Surveys (price still #1 objection at 55%), Site Search ("sale" still in top 3), Intelligems (RPV down 11.8%). Warehouse sale inflated prior period metrics. Now traffic has normalized to full-price buyers, and conversion is struggling. Every test this month should focus on converting full-price visitors.

**Theme B: Price/Value Perception Gap (6 sources)**
Surveys (55% cite price), Reviews (post-purchase love: "feels amazing," "like a special occasion"), Site Search ("eco satin" 672 searches), Heatmaps (reviews with value proof buried on PDP), Competitors (Lake fabric hero, D&D print stories, Quince price transparency), Case Studies (storytelling module +$130K/mo). The gap between pre-purchase skepticism and post-purchase satisfaction is the single biggest conversion opportunity.

**Theme C: Sizing Anxiety Amplified by Final Sale (4 sources)**
Surveys ("lack of garment measurements," "unclear on new sizing"), Reviews ("1-2 sizes too big, no returns"), Competitors (Lake reviews show weight/height/body shape), Case Studies (PDP size selection +$386K/mo). PF23 and PF24 are running on PDP to address this. New nuance: sizing varies not just across fabrics but across silhouettes within the same fabric.

**Theme D: Gifting Opportunity Untapped (4 sources)**
Reviews (94 gift mentions), Content Calendar (Mother's Day focus, "Giftableness" theme), Ads (Bagheera ad sells gifting, LP has no gifting context), Site Screenshots (gift wrapping exists but buried). April is the peak gifting ramp to Mother's Day (May 11). No dedicated gifting experience exists on site.

**Theme E: Print Discovery Still Broken (3 sources)**
Site Search (41K searches dominated by print names), Collection Screenshots (print portals exist on /collections/pajamas but limited), Inspiration (D&D "Shop the Print" on PDP). Print portals are a step forward, but the PDP has no cross-sell by print.

**Theme F: Landing Page Message Match (3 sources)**
Ads (all 3 top ads have message match gaps), Shopify (Bagheera 52K sessions, MacKenzie-Childs 43K), Collection Screenshots (generic grids with no story above fold). Same gaps as March. Client wants more landing page tests.

**Theme G: Knits Launch = New Fabric Education Moment (2 sources)**
Content Calendar ("Focus on new Soft KNIT PJS!! Luna Jersey is the Rock Star"), Non-Data Context (client priorities). A new fabric category is launching April 2. The "Eco Satin sounds sus" problem could repeat with Luna Jersey/Knits if there's no fabric education at launch.

**Theme H: Free Shipping Threshold Pain (3 sources)**
Intelligems (53.3% of orders don't qualify, median order $174 vs. $195 threshold), Surveys ("Having to pay for shipping on a $150 product"), Competitors (highest threshold in category). PF16 and PF22 are testing this. Not actionable for April tests, but informs cart and checkout strategy.
