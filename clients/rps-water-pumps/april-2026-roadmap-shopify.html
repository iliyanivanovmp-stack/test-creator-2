Executive Summary

RPS is growing: sales and AOV are both up month over month. The traffic is there, the product quality is there, the reviews are strong. But heatmaps, search data, and behavioral analytics show clear friction points in how visitors browse products, navigate the site, and move through the purchase flow. These four tests target the highest-impact components across the funnel based on where the data shows the biggest gaps between visitor intent and site experience.

April Testing Slots
01
A/B Test Homepage

Homepage Top 2 Folds

The homepage is the #1 revenue page, but heatmaps show 40% of mobile visitors leave before scrolling past the hero, and 50% are gone by fold 2. Every element above the fold leads with discounts, despite survey data showing service is the #1 purchase driver above price. The strongest trust assets (reviews, BBB rating, expert support) are invisible on the page where most visitors land.

02
A/B Test All product detail pages

PDP Product Gallery

The gallery is the most interacted-with component on every PDP. Heatmaps show the top 3 tapped/clicked elements on both mobile and desktop are all gallery thumbnails. But the current layout scales poorly: products with 20-30+ images create a wall of tiny thumbnails with no sequential browsing on desktop and no image counter on mobile. Visitors are actively engaging with the gallery, but the format is working against them.

May Testing Slots
03
A/B Test Sitewide (header navigation)

Sitewide Navigation

The hamburger menu is the #2 most-tapped element on mobile, and search is the #1 clicked element on desktop. Visitors are actively trying to find products, but the current menu is text-only with no images or guided entry points. Site search data shows visitors searching for products that exist in the navigation but can't be found through browsing alone. Understanding how users best discover products is key to improving the path to purchase.
* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.

04
A/B Test Sitewide (post add-to-cart)

Cart Interaction

Nearly half of visitors who add to cart never reach checkout. The current cart interaction uses an unconventional top-down dropdown format that takes up half the screen. At an AOV above $700, the moment after someone adds to cart is the highest-intent point in the funnel, and the current format may be creating unnecessary friction right when it matters most.
