## Test #1: Ponds & Fountains Landing Page Message Match

**Page:** Ponds & Fountains Collection ([/collections/ponds-and-fountains](https://www.rpswaterpumps.com/collections/ponds-and-fountains))

**Hypothesis:** If we align the collection page hero with what Meta ads promise, we'll increase engagement time beyond 24 seconds and generate revenue from paid social traffic, because visitors currently bounce within seconds when the page doesn't deliver the "30% Off" and before/after transformation the ads set up.

**Data:** 3 of the top 5 Meta ads by spend promise "30% Off Pond Aerators" with before/after pond transformation imagery, but the collection page shows no discount and no before/after visuals (Source: Ads). GA4 confirms the damage: Ponds & Fountains averages 24 seconds engagement time with $0 revenue and just 0.64% of key events in the last 28 days, while comparable pages like the One and Done Kit PDP hold visitors for 1m 48s and generate $8K+ (Source: GA4). Facebook sends roughly 3K sessions per month but produces $0 in social referrer sales (Source: Shopify Analytics). A similar test on a bare collection page that added a hero banner, review badges, and contextual labels produced +$114,870/month in revenue lift (Source: Winning Tests Library).

**V1:** Replace the current hero ("Healthier Ponds with Chemical-Free Aeration") with a hero that matches the ad promise. New headline: "30% Off Pond Aerators" with subtext "Chemical-Free Aeration for Crystal Clear Water." Background image uses the same before/after pond transformation visual from the carousel ad (murky brown pond on the left, crystal clear blue pond on the right). A single green CTA button reads "Shop Aerators." Everything else on the page stays the same. On mobile, the before/after images stack vertically with "BEFORE" and "AFTER" labels. On desktop, they sit side by side behind the headline.
**Brief:** Design: create a split-image hero (desktop: side-by-side, mobile: stacked) using the before/after pond photos from the Meta ad carousel. Headline "30% Off Pond Aerators" in white bold text, left-aligned on desktop, centered on mobile. Subtext "Chemical-Free Aeration for Crystal Clear Water" in smaller white text below. Green CTA button "Shop Aerators" anchors directly to the product grid (smooth scroll). Dev: replace the existing hero section markup. Ensure the 30% discount is reflected in the actual product pricing or shown as a compare-at price on the product cards. If the discount is not active, coordinate with the client to activate it before launch. Hero height: 400px desktop, 300px mobile.

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus two changes to the product cards below: (1) add the store's star rating and review count badge to each product card ("4.86/5 from 531 reviews"), and (2) add a contextual use-case label to each product. "Proud Pond Aerator" gets "Best Seller, Ponds Up to 1 Acre." "Floating Pond Fountain Pump" gets "Best for Large Ponds, 1+ Acre." "Weighted Air Hose" and "Weighted Diffuser Disc" get "Add-On Accessory." Labels appear as small colored pills above the product title on each card.
**Brief:** Design: create pill-shaped labels (rounded rectangle, green background for "Best Seller" products, gray for "Add-On Accessory") positioned at the top-left of each product card image. Font: 11px bold uppercase white text. Star rating badge: 5 gold stars with "4.86 (531 reviews)" in 12px text, positioned below the product title on each card. On mobile, pills and ratings scale proportionally within the 2-column grid. Dev: labels are hardcoded per product (not dynamic). Star rating pulls from the existing Judge.me widget data if possible, otherwise hardcode "4.86/5 from 531 reviews." Remove the left sidebar "Which Pump is Right for Me?" navigation on this page only, as it links to well pump categories and is irrelevant to pond aerator shoppers.

## Test #2: One and Done Kit PDP Social Proof

**Page:** One and Done Well Poly Drop Pipe Kit ([/products/one-and-done-easy-well-pump-install](https://www.rpswaterpumps.com/products/one-and-done-easy-well-pump-install))

**Hypothesis:** If we surface social proof and DIY savings directly above the Add to Cart button, we'll increase add-to-cart rate on the highest-revenue product page, because the current page buries 531 reviews at the bottom and never quantifies the $2K-$8K customers save vs. professional installation.

**Data:** The One and Done Kit is the #1 product by revenue at ~$42K in the last 30 days with a 60% sell-through rate, and its PDP is the #2 landing page with 2,464 sessions and the highest engagement time of any product page at 1m 48s (Source: Shopify Analytics, GA4). Despite this, the page has no star rating near the product title, no social proof near the Add to Cart button, and customer reviews are buried below 10+ content sections (Source: Screenshots). Reviews repeatedly cite DIY savings of $2,000-$8,000 vs. professional installation, with quotes like "Quotes ran from $6k-$9k. We did it all for well under $2k" and "Saved over $8,000" (Source: Reviews). The customer survey found service is the #1 purchase decision driver at 4.3/5, and website performance shapes how customers interpret convenience, price, and service with a correlation of r=0.771 (Source: Survey). A test adding Instagram-stories-style customer photos above the ATC button on a similar niche technical B2C product page produced +$6,635/month (Source: Winning Tests Library).

**V1:** Add a social proof block directly above the Add to Cart button. The block contains: (1) a row of 4 circular customer installation photo thumbnails with colored ring borders (styled like Instagram story indicators), showing real customers next to their installed well pump setups, sourced from review photos already on the site, and (2) a text line below the photos: "Trusted by 500+ DIY homeowners. 4.86/5 from 531 reviews." Clicking any photo thumbnail opens a lightbox showing the full customer photo and their review excerpt. Nothing else on the page changes. On mobile, the 4 circular thumbnails display in a single scrollable row. On desktop, they sit in a static row.
**Brief:** Design: 4 circular photo thumbnails, 48px diameter on mobile, 56px on desktop. Each has a 2px gradient ring (orange to green, matching RPS brand colors). Positioned in a horizontal row with 8px gaps, left-aligned on desktop, centered on mobile. Below the row: "Trusted by 500+ DIY homeowners" in 14px bold text, followed by 5 gold stars and "4.86/5 from 531 reviews" in 13px regular text. The entire block sits directly above the ATC button with 16px margin above and below. Dev: source 4 customer photos from existing Judge.me review photos (prioritize photos showing completed installations). Lightbox on click shows the photo at full size with 2-3 lines of the associated review text. On mobile, the photo row is touch-scrollable if needed but 4 photos at 48px should fit in most viewports.

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus add a "DIY Savings" banner between the product images and the variant selector. The banner reads: "Average customer saves $5,000+ vs. professional installation" with a small "See how" link that smooth-scrolls to a comparison breakdown further down the page. The comparison section (new, added above the existing "DIY Education Channel" section) is a simple 2-column table: "DIY with One and Done Kit" vs. "Professional Well Company" comparing line items: Pump ($X vs. included in quote), Drop pipe kit ($X vs. included), Labor ($0 vs. $3,000-$6,000), Total ($X vs. $6,000-$10,000+). Use actual kit pricing for the left column. On mobile, the banner spans full width with text centered. The comparison table stacks into a single column with headers.
**Brief:** Design: savings banner is a light green background strip (#E8F5E9), full width, 48px height on desktop, auto-height on mobile. Text "Average customer saves $5,000+ vs. professional installation" in 14px bold dark green text, centered. "See how" is an underlined link in regular weight. Comparison table: 2 columns, clean borders, green header row. Left column header: "DIY with RPS" with a checkmark icon. Right column header: "Hire a Pro." Line items: Pump + Motor + Starter Box, Drop Pipe Kit, Wire & Fittings, Labor, Total. Left column uses actual product pricing from the selected variant. Right column uses ranges based on review data ($3,000-$6,000 for labor, $6,000-$10,000+ total). Dev: the savings banner is a static HTML block. The comparison table left-column pricing should pull from the selected variant price dynamically if possible, otherwise hardcode for the most popular variant. Position the comparison section directly above the existing "DIY Education Channel" video section.

## Test #3: Submersible Well Pump PDP Gallery & Trust

**Page:** Submersible well pump PDPs, starting with the 1HP 07RPS10 ([/products/rps-1hp-07rps10](https://www.rpswaterpumps.com/products/rps-1hp-07rps10-up-to-400ft-head-5-to-13gpm-deep-well-submersible-pump-end-1hp-220v-motor-starter-box)). Apply winning variation to all submersible pump PDPs.

**Hypothesis:** If we add a value-proposition text overlay and human context to the first product gallery image, we'll increase add-to-cart rate on submersible pump pages, because the current gallery shows a single white-background pump photo with no context, while 47% of customers cite replacement as their primary pain point and need confidence they're choosing the right pump for their situation.

**Data:** Submersible well pumps are the core product category with 173+ site searches in the last 30 days combining "submersible well pump" (83), "deep well submersible pump" (45), "3 wire submersible well pump" (24), and "well pump" (21) (Source: Site Search). The current PDP shows a single pump on a neutral background with no text overlay, no human element, and no installation context above the fold. The "Take a Closer Look" section with real-world photos sits far below (Source: Screenshots). Customer knowledge is moderate at 3.1/5 and familiarity with RPS is only 3.7/5 (Source: Survey), meaning first-time visitors need immediate reassurance they're in the right place. Competitors like Aqua Science display BBB badges, price match guarantees, and 3,100+ third-party reviews prominently (Source: Competitors). A test optimizing product gallery images with text overlays and human context on a similar niche technical B2C product produced +$15,824/month, with the key finding that "more human context = better performance" (Source: Winning Tests Library). Reviews consistently praise stainless steel construction ("Bugatti Chiron of well seals," "beautifully machined") and DIY ease ("my 7 year old son and I installed the pump 300 feet down") (Source: Reviews).

**V1:** Replace the first product gallery image (white-background pump) with a composite image that includes: (1) the existing pump photo positioned on the right third, and (2) a dark overlay on the left two-thirds containing white text: "All Stainless Steel" (large, bold), "Rated for 15+ Years | 24-Month Warranty" (medium), and "USA-Based Engineer Support" (medium, with a small phone icon). The remaining gallery images stay the same. On mobile, the text overlay sits above the pump image as a stacked layout rather than side-by-side, keeping text legible at small sizes. On desktop, the text and pump image share a single wide frame.
**Brief:** Design: create a 1200x800px hero gallery image for desktop. Dark charcoal background (#1A1A1A) on the left 60%, product pump cutout on the right 40% against a slightly lighter background. Text block left-aligned on the dark section: "All Stainless Steel" in 32px bold white, "Rated for 15+ Years | 24-Month Warranty" in 18px regular white below, "USA-Based Engineer Support" in 18px regular white with a small white phone icon inline. For mobile, create a 800x1000px version: text block centered at top (dark background), pump image below. Dev: replace the first image in the Shopify product gallery. This is a static image swap, not a code change. Create one desktop and one mobile version. Shopify's responsive image handling should serve the appropriate size. Apply the same treatment to all submersible pump PDPs (same text, swap in each product's pump photo).

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus add a "Why RPS?" comparison strip directly below the product gallery (above the product description). The strip is a single horizontal row with 3 comparison columns: "RPS" vs. "Big Box Store" vs. "Hire a Pro." Each column shows: Price (RPS actual price vs. "$250-$500 pump only" vs. "$3,000-$8,000 installed"), Support ("Lifetime engineer support" vs. "Generic call center" vs. "None after install"), Warranty ("24-month full warranty" vs. "1-year limited" vs. "Varies"), and Material ("All stainless steel" vs. "Plastic/cast iron" vs. "Depends on installer"). RPS column is highlighted with a green background. On mobile, the comparison collapses into a vertical list with RPS values shown first with green checkmarks, then competitor values shown as gray strikethrough text below each item.
**Brief:** Design: horizontal strip, full-width, light gray background (#F5F5F5), 12px padding. 3 equal columns. RPS column: light green background (#E8F5E9), bold column header "RPS Water Pumps" with logo. "Big Box Store" column: white background, header in gray. "Hire a Pro" column: white background, header in gray. Each row: label in 11px uppercase gray, value in 14px regular black (or green bold for RPS). For mobile: single-column layout. Each comparison point shows the RPS value first (green checkmark + bold text), then "vs. Big Box: [value]" and "vs. Pro Install: [value]" in 12px gray text below. Dev: static HTML/CSS block injected below the product gallery using Shopify's product page template. Pricing in the RPS column should ideally pull from the product variant, otherwise hardcode per product. The strip is a reusable section that can be added to all submersible pump PDPs with minimal per-page customization (only the RPS price changes).
