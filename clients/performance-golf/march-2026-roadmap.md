# March 2026 Testing Roadmap

**Data Sources:** Shopify Analytics, Meta Ads, Site Search, Reviews (Trustpilot + Testimonials), Competitor Research, Winning Tests Library, Site Screenshots

## Insights

Performance Golf's biggest problem isn't traffic. It's trust. Across 35+ recent Trustpilot reviews, the same complaint surfaces again and again: customers buy a club and discover unwanted subscription charges on their card weeks later. "SCAM-SCAM-SCAM," wrote one buyer. "Tricking elderly people into committing to a monthly fee," wrote another. These aren't isolated incidents. They're a pattern that poisons every stage of the funnel.

That trust deficit shows up in the data. Site search reveals "login" as the #1 zero-result query in the last 30 days (8 searches), with "orders" (3) and "how do i cancel membership?" also appearing. People are looking for account access and cancellation options before they're even ready to buy. Meanwhile, the cart drawer, the last touchpoint before checkout, offers zero reassurance: no trust signals, no guarantee callout, no social proof. Just empty white space and a checkout button.

The irony is that Performance Golf has the strongest guarantee in the competitive set: 365 days, no questions asked. Competitors (BombTech, Vertical Groove, Lag Shot, Square Strike) max out at 30-60 days. PG also has 800K+ clubs sold, 1M+ subscribers, 3,700+ reviews on the 357 alone, and a 4.8 average rating. These are powerful trust signals. They're just buried.

The homepage compounds the problem. It receives 10,549 sessions in the last 30 days, 47.6% of all store traffic, yet the hero promotes the ONE.1 Wedge ($22.5K/mo revenue). The 357 Super 7-Wood generated $145K in the same period. The bestseller has no hero presence on the highest-traffic page. The homepage also tries to do everything: wedge promotion, brand story, app trial, product grid, training content, and golf schools, all competing for attention in the first 3 scrolls.

The collection page has a similar gap. Fourteen products, no reviews on cards, no hero banner, no urgency labels. The 357 has 3,700+ reviews but the collection page shows only a product shot and price. Visitors have no signal for which product is most popular or most trusted.

Conservative estimate: the homepage alone at 10,549 sessions/mo, if a hero test lifts conversion rate by 0.5 percentage points at $166.35 AOV, that's roughly $8,770/mo in additional revenue. Across all four tests targeting the store's highest-traffic pages and universal touchpoints, the combined opportunity is $15,000-$25,000/mo.

## Test #1: Homepage Hero

**Page:** Homepage (https://shop.performancegolf.com/)
**Revenue potential:** 10,549 sessions/mo x 0.5% CR lift x $166.35 AOV = ~$8,770/mo

**Hypothesis:** If we replace the ONE.1 Wedge hero with the 357 Super 7-Wood (the bestseller) and surface trust signals above the fold, more homepage visitors will engage with the top-revenue product and convert, because the current hero promotes the 4th-best seller while the 357 drives 42% of gross sales ($145K of $338K in the last 30 days).

**Data:** The 357 Super 7-Wood generated ~$145K in the last 30 days, more than 3x the next product (Source: Shopify Analytics). The homepage receives 10,549 of the store's 22,128 sessions, 47.6% of all traffic (Source: Shopify Analytics). Currently, the hero promotes the ONE.1 Wedge ($22.5K/mo), a product generating 6.5x less revenue. Trust signals like "4.8 rating," "1M+ subscribers," and "800K clubs sold" exist on the page but are buried in fold 2-3, invisible to visitors who bounce before scrolling (Source: Site Screenshots). Competing in a case study, replacing a vague hero headline with clear product identification drove +$19.4K/mo, and adding a storytelling experience module drove +$130.2K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library).

**V1:** Replace the ONE.1 Wedge hero with the 357 Super 7-Wood. Headline: "The #1 Fairway Wood in Golf" (or similar claim backed by sales data). Subhead pulls from customer language: "3,700+ golfers rate it 4.8 stars." Prominent CTA: "SHOP THE 357." Below the hero, retain the existing stats bar (4.8 AVG RATING, 1M+ SUBSCRIBERS, 800K CLUBS SOLD) but move it directly under the hero so it's visible in fold 1 on both desktop and mobile, rather than fold 2-3. Keep all content below the stats bar unchanged.
**Brief:** Desktop: Full-width hero image of the 357 club (use existing product photography or the golf course lifestyle shot from the 357 LP). Headline left-aligned on desktop, overlaid on image. Stats bar moves from its current position (below the "LOVE YOUR GAME" section) to directly below the hero banner. Mobile: Hero image takes full viewport width. Headline and CTA stack vertically. Stats bar sits immediately below the hero, above "LOVE YOUR GAME." No other sections change position or content.

**V2:** Same 357 hero as V1, plus replace the "LOVE YOUR GAME" brand explainer section (fold 2) with a "Why 800K Golfers Trust Performance Golf" social proof block. This block contains three short customer quotes pulled from Trustpilot reviews with real names, formatted as a horizontal carousel on desktop and a vertical stack on mobile. Below the quotes, the existing stats bar (4.8 rating, 1M+ subscribers, 800K clubs sold). This stacks the 357 hero + social proof + stats before any other content, prioritizing trust and the bestseller.
**Brief:** Desktop: Social proof block replaces the "LOVE YOUR GAME" / "WRONG ORDER = CONFUSION" section entirely. Three review cards in a row, each showing: star rating, one-sentence quote, reviewer name. Use real Trustpilot quotes (e.g., Jeff Kokinacis: "I absolutely love the club. The feel of the ball off the club face is amazing." Big TV Guy: "Knocked about 5 strokes off my handicap. Worth every cent!"). Stats bar directly below. Mobile: Same block, but review cards stack vertically (one per row). Stats bar below. Everything from PG1 section onward remains unchanged in both V1 and V2.

## Test #2: Collection Page Trust + Navigation

**Page:** Golf Clubs collection (https://shop.performancegolf.com/collections/all-clubs)
**Revenue potential:** Estimated 3,000+ internal sessions/mo (the collection page appears in the main nav under "CLUBS" and serves as the primary shopping grid) x 1% ATC rate lift x $166.35 AOV = ~$4,990/mo

**Hypothesis:** If we add a hero banner, star ratings on product cards, and "Best Seller" labels to the collection page, more shoppers will click into product pages and add to cart, because the current page offers no guidance on which of the 14 products to choose and no trust signals to overcome purchase hesitation.

**Data:** The collection page currently shows no reviews, no ratings, no hero, and no urgency labels (Source: Site Screenshots). The 357 has 3,700+ reviews and the SF1 has strong sales ($44.5K in 30 days), but neither is differentiated on the collection grid (Source: Shopify Analytics, Site Screenshots). Site search shows visitors actively look for specific products by name: "357" (10), "driver" (6), "sf1" (5), "359" (3) (Source: Site Search). In a case study, adding a hero banner + review counts + urgency labels to a bare collection page generated +$114.8K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library). The collection page footer has three trust icons (365-Day Guarantee, Free Shipping, Secure Checkout) but they appear only after scrolling past all 14 products (Source: Site Screenshots).

**V1:** Add star ratings and review counts to each product card (e.g., "4.8 | 3,700+ reviews" under the product name for the 357). No other changes. This isolates the impact of social proof alone.
**Brief:** Desktop: Below each product name and price, add a line showing the star rating (filled stars icon) and review count in small text. Use the same style as the 357 PDP (gold stars + "4.8 based on 3,700+ happy reviews" but condensed to "4.8 | 3,700+ reviews"). Apply to all products that have reviews. If a product has no reviews, show nothing (do not show "0 reviews"). Mobile: Same placement, below product name and price on each card. Font size should match the price text.

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus add a hero banner at the top of the collection page and "Best Seller" / "Most Popular" labels on the top 2-3 products. Hero banner: simple lifestyle image (golfer on course) with headline "America's #1 Game Improvement Clubs" and subtext "365-Day Money-Back Guarantee. Free Shipping. 800K+ Clubs Sold." Labels appear as small badges on the 357 ("Best Seller") and SF1 ("Most Popular").
**Brief:** Desktop: Hero banner spans full width above the filter/sort bar. Lifestyle image background (use existing golf course imagery from the homepage or 357 LP). Text overlay left-aligned: headline in bold, subtext in regular weight. Height: approximately 200px, enough for context without pushing products too far down. "Best Seller" and "Most Popular" badges: small pill-shaped labels, positioned on the product card next to the existing "Save up to X%" badge. Use a distinct color (e.g., dark green or charcoal) to differentiate from the beige sale badge. Mobile: Hero banner full-width, stacked text (headline above subtext), approximately 150px height. Badges appear above the product image, same row as the sale badge.

**Sequencing note:** This test can run concurrently with Test #1 (Homepage Hero) since they target different pages. However, if homepage changes significantly alter the traffic mix to the collection page, consider staggering by 2 weeks.

## Test #3: Cart Drawer Trust + Savings

**Page:** Cart drawer (site-wide, appears on all pages)
**Revenue potential:** 661 ATC sessions/mo x 5% checkout completion lift x $166.35 AOV = ~$5,490/mo (plus potential AOV lift from upsells in V2)

**Hypothesis:** If we add trust signals and savings visibility to the cart drawer, more shoppers who add to cart will complete checkout, because the current cart drawer is bare (no trust signals, no savings callout, no social proof) and Trustpilot reviews reveal that hidden charges and subscription fears are the #1 purchase barrier for this brand.

**Data:** The cart drawer currently contains only: free shipping bar, product line item, "Is this a gift?" accordion, and a checkout button, with large empty space between the product and subtotal (Source: Site Screenshots). Trustpilot reviews from the last 30 days show 6+ one-star reviews citing unwanted subscription charges, hidden fees, and difficulty canceling (Source: Reviews). "Login" is the #1 zero-result site search (8 searches), and "how do i cancel membership?" also appears, indicating trust anxiety even before purchase (Source: Site Search). PG's 365-day guarantee is 6x longer than the competitive average of 60 days (Source: Competitor Research). In a case study, adding savings visibility + trust signals to a cart drawer generated +$32.6K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library). In a separate case study, upsells without trust badges outperformed upsells with trust badges for an established brand, generating +$33.1K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library).

**V1:** Add three elements to the cart drawer: (1) A savings callout line below the product showing "You're saving $50!" (calculated from the compare-at price), (2) A trust signal strip below the product section with three icons in a row: "365-Day Guarantee," "Free Shipping," and "800K+ Clubs Sold," (3) Replace the "Shipping, taxes, and discount codes are calculated at checkout" text with "Free shipping included. 365-day money-back guarantee." Remove the "Is this a gift?" accordion (low-value feature consuming premium space).
**Brief:** Desktop: Savings callout appears in green text directly below the product's price line. Trust signal strip: three small icons with text labels in a horizontal row, centered, placed between the product section and subtotal. Use the same icon style as the PDP trust icons (circular outline icons). The "Is this a gift?" accordion is removed. The reassurance text replaces the existing shipping/tax disclaimer below the CHECK OUT button. Mobile: Identical layout. The trust strip icons scale to fit the narrower drawer width. If they don't fit in one row, stack as 2+1.

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus add a "Golfers Also Bought" upsell section with one complementary product recommendation. For example, if the cart contains the 357, suggest the 359 Super 9-Wood ("Complete your set"). If the cart contains the SF1 Driver, suggest the EZ7 hybrid. Show product image, name, sale price with strikethrough, and an "Add" button. Place this section between the cart item and the trust strip.
**Brief:** Desktop: "Golfers Also Bought" section shows one product card: small product image (left), product name + "Complete your set" tagline + sale price with strikethrough (center), "ADD" button (right). The recommendation logic: 357 suggests 359, SF1 suggests EZ7, ONE Wedge suggests ONE.S Wedge, default suggests 357 (bestseller). Mobile: Same layout but product image shrinks, and the ADD button moves below the product name/price to prevent horizontal cramping. Only show one upsell product to keep the drawer focused.

**Sequencing note:** This test runs on a different component (cart drawer) than Tests #1 and #2, so it can run concurrently with both.

## Test #4: 357 PDP Gallery + Customer Voice

**Page:** 357 Super 7-Wood PDP (https://shop.performancegolf.com/products/357-fairway-wood)
**Revenue potential:** 1,060 landing sessions/mo + estimated 2,000+ internal sessions from homepage and collection page x 0.5% ATC rate lift x $166.35 AOV = ~$2,495/mo

**Hypothesis:** If we replace the standard product gallery images with text-overlay action shots and add customer-voice language to the benefit bullets, more PDP visitors will add to cart, because the current gallery uses plain product photography that doesn't communicate the value proposition, and brand-voice benefits ("Eliminate frustrating tops and chunks") are less persuasive than specific customer outcomes ("I gained 30 yards to my drives in 2 days").

**Data:** The 357 PDP gallery currently shows 4+ standard product-shot thumbnails on a neutral background with no text overlays and no human context (Source: Site Screenshots). The product has 3,700+ reviews at 4.8 stars and is the #1 seller at ~$145K in the last 30 days (Source: Shopify Analytics). Customer reviews provide specific, quantifiable outcomes: "I gained 30 yards to my drives in 2 days" (John Golf), "The feel of the ball off the club face is amazing" (Jeff Kokinacis, Trustpilot), "I'm able to use it from different distances" (Jeff Kokinacis) (Source: Reviews). In a case study, replacing a static product shot with a text overlay + hands-on action shot increased revenue by +$15.8K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library). In a separate case study, replacing brand-voice product copy with customer-voice duration/longevity language drove +$50.9K/mo (Source: Winning Tests Library).

**V1:** Replace the first gallery image (the main image visible on load) with a text-overlay version. Keep the same product shot but add a bold headline overlay: "The #1 Fairway Wood in Golf" with a subline: "4.8 Stars. 3,700+ Reviews." This turns the gallery into a persuasion tool rather than a display tool. All other gallery images remain unchanged.
**Brief:** Desktop: The first gallery image gets a semi-transparent dark gradient overlay (bottom 30% of image) with white text. Headline "The #1 Fairway Wood in Golf" in bold, subline "4.8 Stars. 3,700+ Reviews." in regular weight below. Text is left-aligned, positioned in the bottom-left corner of the image. The overlay should not obscure the club head. Remaining thumbnail images stay as-is. Mobile: Same treatment. The text scales proportionally. Ensure the overlay text is legible on the smaller viewport (minimum 16px body text). Thumbnail strip below the main image remains unchanged.

**V2:** Everything from V1, plus replace the five brand-voice benefit bullets with customer-voice versions. Current bullets use brand language ("Eliminate frustrating tops and chunks," "Gain distance, reach more greens"). Replace with specific customer outcomes sourced from reviews, keeping the same structure (checkmark + bold lead-in + supporting text). Example replacements: "Gain 20-30 extra yards without swinging harder" (from John Golf's testimonial), "Flush it clean from the rough, the fairway, even fairway bunkers" (from the 357 LP use-case demos), "Stop guessing which club to pull from 150-200 yards" (retained, already strong), "Feel the satisfying 'tick' of flush contact, even on mis-hits" (retained, strong sensory language), "3,700+ golfers rate it 4.8 stars, the #1 game-improvement wood in golf" (new, social proof as a benefit).
**Brief:** Desktop: Replace the five bullet items in the product description section (below the "The First Club With Tri-Fusion Technology..." bold subheading). Keep the same layout: red checkmark icon, bold lead-in phrase, supporting text. Only the copy changes, not the structure or styling. Mobile: Same copy changes. The bullet layout already stacks well on mobile (full-width, one bullet per row). No structural changes needed.

**Sequencing note:** If Test #1 (Homepage Hero) drives significantly more traffic to the 357 PDP, wait 2 weeks after Test #1 launches before starting Test #4 to establish a stable traffic baseline.
