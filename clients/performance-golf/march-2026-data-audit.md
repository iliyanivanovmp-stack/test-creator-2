## Site Screenshots Analysis (Desktop + Mobile)

### Homepage
- **Hero promotes ONE.1 Wedge**, the 4th-best-selling product ($22.5K/mo). The #1 product (357 at $145K/mo) has no hero presence.
- **Strong stats (4.8 rating, 1M+ subscribers, 800K clubs sold) buried in fold 2-3.** First-time visitors don't see these trust signals.
- **PG1 app section with "START YOUR FREE 7-DAY TRIAL"** is prominent. Given the subscription billing complaints from reviews, this trial CTA may create anxiety rather than reduce it.
- **Multiple sections compete for attention:** Wedge hero, LOVE YOUR GAME explainer, PG1 app, product grid, Auto-Correct Wedge, testimonials, 357 section, Click Stick, training content, VIP Schools, footer. The page tries to do everything.

### 357 PDP
- **Above the fold is well-structured on mobile.** Product gallery, price with strikethrough, 4.8 stars / 3,700+ reviews, IN STOCK badge, benefit bullets, variant selectors, ATC with price, delivery estimate, trust icons.
- **Below the fold has extensive content:** Tri-Fusion Technology explanation, bonus reasons, $885 in bonuses, 365-day guarantee badge, senior golfer testimonials, tech specs (Dual Rails, Raised Rear Sole), FAQ.
- **Gallery images are standard product shots.** No text overlays, no action shots, no human context. Case study showed product gallery with text overlay + hands-on action shot = +$15.8K/mo.

### Cart Drawer
- **Critically bare.** Free shipping bar (already qualified), product details, "Is this a gift?" accordion, massive empty space, subtotal, CHECK OUT button.
- **No trust signals.** Given the subscription billing complaints, buyers reaching the cart need reassurance. No mention of 365-day guarantee, no "800K+ clubs sold," no Trustpilot rating.
- **No upsells.** No "Frequently Bought Together," no complementary products.
- **No savings visibility.** Shows $249 / ~~$299~~ but doesn't call out "You're saving $50!"
- **"Shipping, taxes, and discount codes are calculated at checkout"** is the last thing buyers see before checkout. This is anxiety-inducing, not reassuring.

### Collection Page (Golf Clubs)
- **No hero banner.** Just "GOLF CLUBS 14" title.
- **No reviews or ratings on product cards.** The 357 has 3,700+ reviews but you'd never know from the collection page.
- **No urgency labels.** No "Best Seller," "Most Popular," or stock indicators.
- **"Save up to X%" badges exist** but are the only differentiating element.
- **3-column grid on desktop, 1-column on mobile.** Mobile shows one oversized product card per screen, requiring excessive scrolling.
- **Trust icons at the bottom** (365-Day Guarantee, Free Shipping, Secure Checkout) but not visible until after all 14 products.
- Case study: adding hero + reviews + urgency to a bare collection page = +$114.8K/mo.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: Trust Deficit (Reviews + Site Search + Cart Drawer + Competitor Gap)
The biggest conversion blocker is trust. Trustpilot reviews show a pattern of unwanted subscription charges. Site search shows people can't find account access or cancel memberships. The cart drawer has zero trust signals. And PG's 365-day guarantee (strongest in the competitive set) is underutilized across the store.

### Theme 2: Homepage Misalignment (Shopify Analytics + Site Screenshots + Ads)
The homepage gets 47.6% of sessions but leads with the 4th-best-selling product. The 357 ($145K/mo revenue, 1,060 direct landing sessions) and SF1 ($44.5K/mo) are the revenue drivers. The homepage also tries to serve too many purposes: product promotion, brand story, app trial, training content, and golf schools.

### Theme 3: Collection Page Gap (Site Screenshots + Case Studies)
The collection page is bare: no hero, no reviews, no urgency. Case studies show this exact pattern (adding hero + reviews + urgency labels) generates massive revenue lifts. With 14 products and prices ranging from $129-$379, helping visitors navigate to the right product matters.

### Theme 4: Cart Drawer as Conversion Leak (Site Screenshots + Reviews + Case Studies)
Every buyer passes through the cart drawer. It's currently empty space. Given the trust concerns from reviews, this is the last chance to reassure before checkout. Case studies show cart drawer trust signals + savings visibility = +$32.6K/mo.
