### Red Flag: Bot Traffic

China sessions: 34,100, up 182% from prior period. This is likely bot traffic and explains much of the session growth (+15%) and conversion rate decline (-9%). Excluding China traffic, estimated real sessions are ~491K with an adjusted CR closer to 0.59%.

### Customer Cohort Analysis

Retention declines steadily month over month. Recent cohorts (Jan 2026: 5.88% month-1, Feb 2026: 6.92% month-1) show consistent first-month return rates. Older cohorts (Jul 2025: 8.38% month-1) had stronger initial retention, suggesting newer customers may be lower quality or less engaged.

### Returns

Returns up 37% to $71,198. At 3.5% of gross sales, this warrants investigation. Could correlate with the seat comfort and assembly complaints surfaced in reviews.

## Competitor Landscape

### Key Competitors

| Brand | Price Range | Positioning |
|---|---|---|
| Traxion ProGear | ~$179 | Budget. 5" polyurethane wheels. Known durability issues (gas shock failures). |
| Harbor Freight | ~$50-100 | Lowest cost. The "why would I spend $600" alternative. |
| Craftsman | ~$100-200 | Mid-range. Brand recognition. |
| DeWalt | ~$100-200 | Adjustable creeper stool. Brand trust from power tools. |
| Safco | ~$200 | Lab stools. Screw-lift mechanism. |

### Competitive Insight
Vyper's primary competitive challenge is price justification. At $500-700, they're 3-4x the price of the next alternative. Forum discussions (Garage Journal, BobIsTheOilGuy) show the audience splits into "buy once, cry once" believers vs. "it's a shop stool" skeptics. Multiple Vyper reviews reference Harbor Freight as the comparison point. The "cost per year" argument is Vyper's strongest counterpoint but it's not used anywhere on the site.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: Price Justification is the #1 Barrier
- **Reviews:** "Nice but not $600 nice," "feels like a $250-300 chair," "could have bought 5 from Harbor Freight"
- **Surveys:** 54% of buyers considered for 3+ months before purchasing, 26.5% waited over a year
- **Competitors:** Vyper is 3-4x the price of alternatives
- **Site:** No "cost per year" comparison, no competitor comparison, no ROI framing anywhere on site

### Theme 2: Collection Page is the Biggest Missed Opportunity
- **Shopify Analytics:** 128,900 sessions/mo, top landing page
- **Ads:** 2 of 3 top ads by spend land here
- **Site:** No hero, no story, no emotional continuity from ads. Just a filter sidebar and product grid.
- **Revenue potential:** At 128.9K sessions, even a 0.1% CR lift = ~$80K/mo additional revenue

### Theme 3: The Funnel Leaks at Cart and Checkout
- **Shopify Analytics:** 28.2% drop from ATC to checkout, 58.2% drop from checkout to purchase
- **Cart drawer:** No trust signals, no delivery estimate, no social proof beyond shipping protection stat
- **AOV:** At $622, this is a high-stakes purchase decision. The cart drawer treats it like a commodity.

### Theme 4: Reviews Surface Objections the Site Doesn't Address
- **Reviews:** Seat comfort, height range, foot ring, assembly difficulty
- **PDP:** "About This Product" is hidden behind an accordion. No proactive FAQ addressing these concerns.
- **Opportunity:** Surfacing answers to the most common objections above the fold could pre-empt hesitation.

### Theme 5: Mobile Dominates but Experience Unknown
- **Shopify Analytics:** 76.2% of traffic is mobile
- **Ads:** Facebook/Instagram ads (mobile-first platforms) drive 186.8K sessions
- **Unknown:** No mobile-specific conversion rate breakdown available. With 76% mobile traffic and a complex 3-step configurator, mobile UX is critical.
