## Added Value: Sitewide Copy Guidelines (Redesign Deliverable)

**Context:** PeachyBbies is doing a full site redesign. This is a copy strategy guide they can hand to their team (and any copywriters/designers) to apply across the new site. Grounded in what we already know works from PB24 Bundle Descriptions testing.

**What we know from PB24 testing (Bundle Descriptions):**
- V2 (Handmade in the USA / Premium Quality) and V3 (Texture/ASMR Enthusiast) won across 4 bundle PDPs.
- Generic convenience copy ("bundle and save," "calming fun") lost.
- Specificity wins. Concrete proof points and sensory language outperform vague value props.

**Deliverable:** Copy guidelines doc (see `march-2026-copy-guidelines.md`)

## Test 1: PB23 - Slime vs. Other Toys Comparison Block (Homepage)

**Page:** Homepage
**Location:** Below "New Slime Drop" section
**Status:** Wireframed, untested. Can carry over to the new site.
**TestBuddy:** https://www.testbuddy.dev/share/2b3b398e-b92a-4958-a712-5fd9cce102f5?test=ac3be4a6-3b4f-45b5-ae84-abfb351d867a
**Variations:** 3

**Hypothesis:** Adding a "Slime vs. Other Toys" comparison block that contrasts slime with common alternatives will reframe slime from "just a toy" to a multi-sensory, collectible, anxiety-relieving product, increasing curiosity and click-through rate.

**Control:** Current homepage (product cards + SMS signup, no comparison content).

**V1: Slime vs. Screen Time.** Comparison table: Slime vs. Mobile Games. Highlights: "Calming sensory focus" (slime yes, mobile no), "Zero blue light, no eye strain" (slime yes), "Offline anywhere, no Wi-Fi or data" (slime yes). Competitor chips: Mobile Games, Video Consoles, Light-up/Sound-FX Toys. CTA: "Shop Sensory Play."

**V2: Slime vs. Textured Toys.** Comparison table: Slime vs. Play-Doh. Highlights: "Smooth, stretchy feel, not crumbly or stiff" (slime yes), "Non-sticky cleanup, easy container storage" (slime yes), "Scented relaxation, soothing themed scents" (slime yes). Competitor chips: Play-Doh, Kinetic Sand, Water Beads. CTA: "Shop Sensory Play."

**V3: Slime vs. Viral Stress-Relief Toys.** Comparison table: Slime vs. Fidget Spinners. Highlights: "Multi-sensory engagement, touch, sight, ASMR" (slime yes), "Creative play options, shape, swirl, blend" (slime yes), "Replay value, never just one motion" (slime yes, fidget spinners no). Competitor chips: Fidget Spinners, Pop-Its, Stress Balls. CTA: "Shop Sensory Play."

**Note:** All three variations position slime as the superior option across different competitor categories. The question is which comparison resonates most with PeachyBbies' audience. Given PB24 data, V2 (textured toys) may perform best since it speaks to the texture-conscious buyer. V1 (screen time) targets parents. V3 (stress-relief) targets the ASMR/adult audience.

## Test 2: PBXX - Gamified PDP Upsells (Product Page)

**Page:** Product Page
**Location:** Below product description
**Status:** Wireframed, untested. Concept can carry over to the new site.
**TestBuddy:** https://www.testbuddy.dev/share/2b3b398e-b92a-4958-a712-5fd9cce102f5?test=c22215e6-deff-4ce7-b5f8-68871dd93f5f
**Variations:** 3

**Hypothesis:** Presenting the existing tiered free gift offer with improved visual hierarchy and gamified "Bundle and Get Free Gifts" messaging will reduce friction and increase AOV by making the purchase path clearer.

**Control:** Plain text list of extras by package size tier (1 slime, 2-4 slimes, 5+ slimes). No product recommendations. No visual differentiation between tiers.

**V1: Product recommendations below tiers.** Keep the tiered extras text, add a scrollable list of recommended products below with "+" add buttons. Makes it easy to add more slimes to hit the next gift tier.

**V2: Grid product recs + add-ins visibility.** Show add-ins and scent info at the top of the section. Product recommendations in a 3-column grid with "Add to Cart" buttons. Tiers listed below.

**V3: Visually gamified tiers.** Each tier (1 slime, 2-4 slimes, 5+ slimes) gets its own colored card with pink background. Tiers become the visual focus. Scent and add-ins info shown at the top. Makes progression from tier to tier feel like unlocking rewards.

**What to watch:** AOV is the primary metric here. CR matters but the real win is getting customers from 1 slime to 2-4 or 5+. Track tier distribution shifts, not just overall conversion.

## Test 3: PBXX - US Founder Trust Block (Homepage)

**Page:** Homepage
**Location:** Top 2 folds
**Status:** Wireframed, untested. Concept can carry over to the new site.
**TestBuddy:** https://www.testbuddy.dev/share/2b3b398e-b92a-4958-a712-5fd9cce102f5?test=3836846b-202f-462b-bd5e-f165b8fcd493
**Variations:** 2

**Hypothesis:** Adding a face to the brand and emphasizing that the slime is handmade in the US will increase trust and perceived value, reducing price hesitation.

**Why this aligns with PB24 data:** V2 (Handmade Quality) won on Bestsellers and Beginner bundles. This test takes that same quality/origin messaging and gives it a permanent home on the homepage with a founder story and photo.

**Control:** Current homepage top 2 folds (product cards + SMS signup).

**V1:** Replace SMS signup section with a founder trust block. Full-width section with photo of Andrea, the founder story ("What started in her apartment with her mom and sister mixing batches by hand has grown into a full 'slime kitchen' in Austin, Texas"), handmade-in-the-USA messaging, signed by "The PeachyBbies team." Pink background.

**V2:** Same founder trust block as V1 with adjusted layout. Text positioned beside the photo with sparkle accents. Same copy and messaging, different visual treatment.

**Key copy from wireframe:**
- "Every slime lover deserves something that sparks joy."
- "We hand-make every single container in Austin, Texas because we believe slime should be more than just gooey fun. It's a moment of calm in your day, a creative outlet, and honestly? Just really satisfying to squish."
- "We're not just making slime, we're making life more fun, one squishy container at a time."

**Note for new site:** This block should be a permanent fixture, not just a test. The PB24 data already proved that handmade/USA messaging converts. The question is whether adding the founder's face and story amplifies it further.
