# PeachyBbies Copy Guidelines for Redesign

These guidelines are based on A/B test data from PB24 Bundle Descriptions. We tested 4 copy angles across 4 bundle PDPs. V2 and V3 won, but on different products. That split tells us when to use which angle.

---

## Which Angle Won Where

| Product | Winner | Angle | Purchase type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bestsellers Bundle | V2 | Handmade Quality | "I know what I want" |
| Weekly NEW Bundle | V3 | Texture/ASMR | "Surprise me" |
| Beginner Bundle | V2 | Handmade Quality | "I know what I want" |
| Subscription Box | V3 | Texture/ASMR | "Surprise me" |

**The insight:** The winning angle depends on the customer's mindset.

**Quality (V2) wins when the customer already knows what they want.** Bestsellers and Beginner are decision-made purchases. The buyer picked a bundle, now they need reassurance it's worth the money. "Crafted in small batches in the USA using premium materials" closes that gap. It justifies the price and builds trust.

**Texture/ASMR (V3) wins when the customer is buying discovery.** Weekly NEW and Subscription are "surprise me" purchases. The buyer doesn't know exactly what they're getting. "Never-before-seen textures and ASMR sensations" creates anticipation. Naming specific textures ("crisp fizz pops to smooth butter kneads") makes the unknown feel exciting, not risky.

**Use this to guide the redesign:** Match the lead angle to the purchase intent on each page.

| Page / context | Lead with | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Individual PDPs | Quality (V2) | Customer picked a specific product. Reassure them. |
| Bundle PDPs (curated) | Quality (V2) | Same as above. They chose this bundle. |
| Weekly drop / new arrivals | Texture/ASMR (V3) | Discovery mode. Make the unknown exciting. |
| Subscription pages | Texture/ASMR (V3) | Ongoing discovery. Sell the anticipation. |
| Collection pages | Texture/ASMR (V3) | Browsing/exploring. Help them feel the difference between textures. |
| Homepage hero | Either, depending on what's being promoted | New drop? V3. Bestseller spotlight? V2. |
| Cart / checkout | Quality (V2) | Decision is made. Reinforce value. |
| Trust bar / footer | Quality (V2) | Always-on reassurance. |

Both angles should appear on most pages. The question is which one leads.

## The Two Winning Angles in Detail

### 1. Handmade Quality (V2)

Lead with craftsmanship. PeachyBbies slimes are handmade in small batches in the USA with premium materials. This isn't mass-produced toy aisle stuff. Make that clear.

**Winning copy from PB24 (real examples):**

| Product (V2 winner) | Headline | Key phrases |
|---|---|---|
| Bestsellers | "Handmade with care, crafted for the perfect feel" | "crafted in small batches in the USA using premium materials" |
| Beginner | "Handmade, soft, and easy to love" | "crafted in small batches in the USA," "premium feel that stretches beautifully" |

**The formula:** Handmade + small batch + USA + tangible benefit the customer can feel.

**Why it works:** Justifies the price point. Differentiates from cheap slime kits. Builds trust. Customers want to know what they're paying for.

### 2. Texture-First / ASMR Language (V3)

Lead with what the slime feels and sounds like. Name each texture specifically. Speak to the ASMR community directly.

**Winning copy from PB24 (real examples):**

| Product (V3 winner) | Headline | Key phrases |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly NEW | "Discover new ASMR textures every week" | "never-before-seen slimes and textures," "crisp fizz pops to smooth butter kneads and glossy pulls" |
| Subscription | "Get ready for your favorite delivery of the month!" | "never-before-seen slimes," "ASMR sensations," "crunchy, creamy, and glossy styles" |

**The formula:** Name textures individually + use sensory language (sounds, feels) + reference ASMR + emphasize newness/exclusivity.

**Why it works:** This is the product. Slime buyers care about texture above everything else. Vague descriptions ("fun to play with") don't help them decide. Specific texture language does.

### Shared element: Texture descriptors in parentheses

Both V2 and V3 added texture descriptors after each slime name in the product list. The control did not.

| Control format | V2/V3 format |
|---|---|
| Baby Axolotl (Snow Fizz) | Baby Axolotl (Snow Fizz, crackly pops, crispy crunch) |
| De-Stress Dough (Butter) | De-Stress Dough (Butter, creamy knead, smooth stretch) |

This small addition lets customers scan the bundle and immediately understand what each texture feels like. Apply this format to every product listing sitewide.

## What Lost (Avoid These Patterns)

### Generic Convenience Copy (V4)

**Examples from PB24 that lost:**
- "All the hits, carefully curated"
- "No scrolling, no second-guessing"
- "The easiest way to start your PeachyBbies collection"
- "No guessing, no waiting on restocks"

This is filler. It describes the format (bundle, convenience) not the product. Customers already know what a bundle is. "Carefully curated" and "no guesswork" could describe any subscription box on the internet.

### Vague Wellness Framing (V1)

**Examples from PB24 that lost:**
- "Screen-free play for kids and ultra-satisfying stress relief for adults"
- "Designed for a calming, mess-free experience"
- "A ritual of calm the whole family will love"
- "Great for sensory relaxation, creative play, or simply taking a quiet moment to unwind"

Too broad. Could describe a candle, a puzzle, or a weighted blanket. Nothing specific to PeachyBbies. The audience already knows slime is satisfying. They want to know which texture is satisfying and how.

## Applying This Sitewide

### Product Descriptions (PDPs)

**Current pattern:** Product name + size + price. Minimal description.

**Recommendation:** Add 2-3 sentences per product combining both winning angles. Lead with texture (V3), close with quality (V2).

**Formula:**
> [Sensory texture description]. [What makes this one different]. [Handmade/quality signal].

**Example (Bestsellers Bundle):**

| Current | Recommended |
|---|---|
| "Whats better than one Peachybbies slime? FIVE PEACHYBBIES SLIMES! We've hand picked a lineup of our most loved slime creations so you don't have to choose just one." | "The crackly crunch of Baby Axolotl, the buttery smoothness of De-Stress Dough, the pillowy drizzles of Lavender Cow, the loud pops of Liquid Fireworks, and the glassy stretches of Ocean Buddies. Five textures that deliver the full range of slime satisfaction. Crafted in small batches in the USA using premium materials." |

The current copy is excitement without substance. The recommended version tells you exactly what you're getting and why it's worth it.

**Example (Subscription Box):**

| Current | Recommended |
|---|---|
| "Get ready for a monthly surprise! Each PeachyBbies Subscription Box features three exclusive slimes inspired by a brand-new theme, complete with fresh textures, vibrant colors, and delightful scents." | "Three limited-edition slimes every month, handcrafted in small batches in the USA. Each box introduces never-before-seen textures and ASMR sensations: expect a balanced mix of crunchy, creamy, and glossy styles, themed and crafted for that month's drop." |

The current copy says "fresh textures, vibrant colors, and delightful scents" (vague). The recommended version says "crunchy, creamy, and glossy styles" (specific) and adds the quality anchor ("handcrafted in small batches in the USA").

### Headlines & Hero Banners

**Current:** Playful, casual, emoji-heavy ("✨you might like these✨").

**Keep:** The playful tone. It's on-brand.
**Add:** A quality or texture anchor. Every headline should include one concrete detail.

Look at the headline shift from PB24. The control headlines were generic ("Bundle and Save!"). The winning headlines were specific:

| Control headline (lost) | V2/V3 headline (won) |
|---|---|
| "Bundle and Save!" | "Handmade with care, crafted for the perfect feel" |
| "This week's slime drop, all in one easy bundle!" | "Freshly handmade, premium textures each week" |
| "Subscribe and Save!" | "Handmade exclusives, delivered every month" |

Apply this same shift sitewide:

| Instead of | Try |
|---|---|
| "New slimes are here!" | "Six new textures, handcrafted this week" |
| "Shop our bestsellers" | "Our five most-loved textures" |
| "Subscribe and save" | "Three exclusive slimes, made just for subscribers. Every month." |

### CTAs

**Current:** "Add," "Shop Now," "See details." Functional but generic.

**Recommendation:** Keep CTAs short but make them texture- or value-specific where possible.

| Page | Current | Option A | Option B |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDP | "Add to cart" | Keep as-is (don't overcomplicate) | Keep as-is |
| Bundle PDP | "Add to cart" | "Grab the bundle" | "Get all [N] textures" |
| Collection page | "Shop Now" | "Explore textures" | "See what's new" |
| Subscription | "Subscribe" | "Get your monthly drop" | "Start your subscription" |

Don't force clever CTAs everywhere. PDPs should stay clean. Bundles and subscriptions have more room to play.

### Navigation & Category Labels

**Current:** "Shop," "Gift Guide," "Slime Care," "Subscribe." Clean and functional.

**Recommendation:** These are fine. Navigation should be scannable, not clever. No changes needed unless the redesign adds new categories.

### Free Gift Tier Messaging

**Current:** "✨1 slime away from more free gifts✨," "Add a Slime to get 4 Gifts."

**Keep this.** The quantified reward messaging is already specific and action-oriented. If anything, add texture language to describe the gifts themselves ("Add one more for a free glossy pull sample").

### Trust Signals / Footer / About

**Recommendation:** This is where the Handmade Quality angle lives permanently:
- "Handcrafted in small batches in the USA"
- "Premium ingredients, made by our team"
- "Shipped fresh from our studio every week"

These should appear in the footer, About page, and anywhere the brand story is told. Repeat them. Customers scroll past things. Redundancy is fine for trust signals.

---

## The One Rule

**Be specific or say nothing.**

Every line of copy should pass this test: *Could this describe a different product?* If yes, rewrite it.

- "Fun for the whole family" could describe anything. Cut it.
- "A crackly, crunchy fizz that melts into buttery cloud dough" can only describe this slime. Keep it.

The PB24 test data is clear: customers respond to copy that tells them exactly what they're getting. Give them textures, materials, origins, and specifics. Skip the fluff.
