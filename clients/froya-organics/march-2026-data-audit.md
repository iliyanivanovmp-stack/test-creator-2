### Message Match Analysis
**Ads promise:** Norse origin story, natural arctic plants, "clear dewy radiant skin in 60 days," money-back guarantee, 7,422+ reviews, customer transformations, specific ingredients (sea buckthorn, arnica, beeswax).

**Landing page hero delivers:** "Delay Wrinkles by 10 years, Guaranteed" with press logos (TeenVogue, Cosmopolitan, marie claire).

**Gap:** The LP hero uses a different headline framing ("delay wrinkles by 10 years") than any of the ads. The Norse/Viking origin story that dominates ad copy is not reflected in the hero. Press logos are a different trust angle than reviews/testimonials. The 60-day guarantee, which every ad mentions, is buried at the bottom of the page.

## Competitor Research

### Pai Skincare (Age Confidence Range)
- Explicitly names menopause in content and SEO
- Clinical data front and center: "95% saw improvement in elasticity"
- Modular boosters ($25-32) allow customization
- 90-day returns (vs. Froya's 60-day)
- B Corp, COSMOS Natural, Leaping Bunny certified
- Unretouched imagery policy

### Tata Harper
- Farm-to-face provenance story (own Vermont farm)
- Heavy review volume (1,033 reviews on single product)
- Multi-image PDPs with texture shots
- Retail distribution (Sephora, Nordstrom) as trust signal
- Per-product entry points ($88-198) rather than system-first

### True Botanicals (Renew Collection)
- Clinical claims: "outperform two top anti-aging serums"
- University research partnerships (Carnegie Mellon, Cornell)
- 5+ certifications stacked (MADE SAFE, EWG, Vegan, Cruelty-Free)
- User-submitted before/after photos with verification
- Subscription at ~27% discount with clear value framing

### Norre Nordic (closest geographic competitor)
- "Skincare Rewilded" positioning
- Wildcrafted from own Finnish garden (specific, tangible sourcing)
- 100% vegan (Froya uses beeswax)
- Small-batch artisanal credibility

### Key Competitive Gaps for Froya
1. No third-party certifications displayed
2. No explicit menopause content/SEO
3. System-first approach creates higher barrier to entry vs. per-product options
4. No head-to-head clinical comparisons
5. Origin/sourcing story less specific than competitors (generic "Arctic" vs. named farms/locations)
6. No unretouched imagery policy

## Winning Tests Library (Convertibles Case Studies)

### Most Relevant Patterns
1. **Cart drawer total on checkout button:** +$389K/month for luxury brand. Showing full price on checkout button eliminates surprise charges.
2. **Brand storytelling module on homepage:** +$130K/month. Story reframes value before price, directly addresses price objection.
3. **PDP buy box restructure:** +$386K/month. Group price + reviews on same line, active CTAs, social proof above fold.
4. **Cart drawer trust signal stack:** +$32K/month. Three-signal stack (reviews + customers + returns) outperformed single signals.
5. **PDP copy with customer voice:** +$50K/month. Leading with customer language outperformed brand claims.
6. **Subscription value breakdown:** +$41K/month. Showing per-item RRP values drove conversion. Transparency about value unlocked lift.
7. **Free gifts vs. discounts:** +$60K/month. Multiple free gifts outperformed percentage discounts.
8. **Press logo bar on mobile:** WARNING. Press quotes lost on mobile despite winning on desktop. With 91.5% mobile traffic, test mobile separately.

## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### Theme 1: Price Objection Is the #1 Barrier (High Confidence)
- 53% of survey respondents cite price/cost (Source: Survey)
- Discounts growing +52% MoM, suggesting heavy discounting to drive conversion (Source: Shopify)
- Competitor comparison shows Froya's system is competitively priced ($119-159 for 4 products) but this isn't communicated (Source: Competitor Research)
- No per-item value breakdown on PDP to justify system pricing (Source: Site Audit)
- Case study: subscription value breakdown drove +$41K/month (Source: Winning Tests)

### Theme 2: Trust Gap Between Ads and Landing Page (High Confidence)
- 9% cite trust/legitimacy as objection, 5% cite AI/ads concerns (Source: Survey)
- Ads promise Norse origin story, reviews, guarantee. LP hero uses different headline. Guarantee buried at bottom. (Source: Ads + Site Audit)
- 41.58% returning customer rate suggests product quality is good, but first-time buyer trust is the bottleneck (Source: Shopify)
- Verbatims: "scam," "fake," "AI generated," "too good to be true" (Source: Survey)
- Case study: brand storytelling module drove +$130K/month by reframing value (Source: Winning Tests)

### Theme 3: Mobile Experience Carrying the Business (High Confidence)
- 91.5% of sessions are mobile (Source: Shopify)
- LP and PDP are extremely long on mobile, requiring extensive scrolling to reach key information (Source: Site Audit)
- Trust signals, guarantee, and pricing are buried deep on mobile (Source: Site Audit)
- Case study warning: press logo bar won on desktop but lost on mobile (Source: Winning Tests)
- Survey: "website is quite messy," "too much going on," "not easy to navigate" (Source: Survey)

### Theme 4: Subscription Model Creates Friction (Medium Confidence)
- 8% of survey respondents cite subscription concerns (Source: Survey)
- Loop Subscriptions growing only +8% vs. +24% for Online Store (Source: Shopify)
- Verbatims show confusion about subscription flexibility, pricing changes, and cancellation (Source: Survey)
- On PDP, one-time purchase is shown first; subscription is secondary (Source: Site Audit)
- Case study: transparent subscription value breakdown drove +$41K/month (Source: Winning Tests)

### Theme 5: Funnel Drop-Off at Checkout (Medium Confidence)
- 52% of checkout visitors don't complete purchase (Source: Shopify)
- 27.5% of ATC visitors don't reach checkout (Source: Shopify)
- Returns up +24% MoM (Source: Shopify)
- Case study: checkout button with price + lock icon drove +$389K/month (Source: Winning Tests)
- Case study: checkout social proof drove +$6K/month (Source: Winning Tests)
