## Cross-Source Analysis: Converging Themes

### 1. Trust Deficit (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Sources: Surveys (7.6%), Trustpilot complaints, third-party reviews, ad message mismatch, site UX (aggressive urgency/discounting)
- Customers explicitly call the site "scammy" and question legitimacy
- Low stock warnings, constant discounts, subscription pressure compound the issue
- Cart drawer has zero trust signals at the moment of purchase

### 2. Landing Page Message Mismatch (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Sources: 3 Meta ads, LP screenshots, email campaigns
- Three different ad angles all land on the same generic hero
- The strongest ad hooks (ingredient story, transformation testimonials, Viking heritage) are absent from fold 1
- Emails prove that clinical data and product matchmaking convert. The LP does neither above the fold.

### 3. Subscription Friction (MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Sources: Surveys (4.5%), Trustpilot (auto-enrollment complaints), PDP/cart UX
- Customers confused about pricing, cancel/pause process
- Subscription pushed aggressively: PDP defaults to highlighting sub, cart drawer has toggle
- Some customers report being enrolled without consent

### 4. Price Objection Without Value Context (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Sources: Surveys (54.1%), email campaign analysis, case study (storytelling module +$130K/mo)
- Price is the #1 objection by far
- Emails frame value through clinical results and transformation stories
- LP and PDP don't do enough value framing before showing price
- Clinical trial data exists but is buried (LP fold 3+, PDP accordion)

### 5. Cart-to-Checkout Drop-off (MEDIUM CONFIDENCE)
Sources: Shopify funnel (4.67% ATC to 1.62% purchase), cart drawer UX, case studies
- Significant conversion leak between ATC and checkout
- Cart drawer lacks trust signals at the decision point
- Case study: stacking trust signals above checkout button drove +$32,605/mo
