Insights

Price is the #1 purchase objection (54.1% of buyers), but the real problem is value perception. Froya's clinical trial data (+79% hydration, +88% radiance) is buried behind an FAQ accordion on the PDP. The emails lead with it. The site doesn't.

7.6% of buyers questioned if the brand was legitimate. The cart drawer, where doubt peaks, has zero trust signals. The main landing page alone gets 1.2M sessions/mo at 1.62% CR. Even a small lift here moves the needle significantly.

Month 2 Focus
High-Impact PDP Tests + Agentic Commerce
Shopify is launching ChatGPT as an agentic storefront channel. Buyers will discover and purchase products directly inside ChatGPT. What the AI surfaces is shaped by product content: gallery images, descriptions, and pricing structure. Three PDP tests to make sure Froya shows up right.
This Month
01
A/B Test Best-seller PDPs

Product Gallery Test

Test an optimized gallery sequence that leads with texture close-ups and application shots instead of the current product-on-white. Skincare buyers at $119 need to see the product's feel and finish before committing. Reorder the gallery to lead with sensory imagery, followed by before/after results, then ingredient hero shots.
* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.
Estimated Launch
Week 1 of Month 2
02
A/B Test Best-seller PDPs

Product Description Test

Test restructured descriptions that lead with clinical outcomes instead of ingredient lists. Replace "contains bakuchiol and squalane" with "79% improvement in hydration in 28 days." Address the core buyer question directly: "Will this actually work for my skin?" Use AI-generated copy grounded in the clinical trial data Froya already has.
* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.
Estimated Launch
Week 2 of Month 2
03
A/B Test Best-seller PDPs

Product Pricing Test

Test price anchoring directly in the price display area. Show $119 alongside a cost-per-day breakdown and comparison to spa facial costs. 54.1% of buyers cite price as the top objection. Reframing "$119 for 90 days = $1.32/day vs. $150+ for a single facial" converts sticker shock into value clarity.

Week 3 of Month 2
 
Month 3 Focus
Product Discovery + SEO Optimization
Capture demand at the discovery layer. A skincare quiz to match visitors to the right product for their concerns, plus structured data markup so AI search tools and Shopify's new agentic storefronts surface Froya when buyers search "best organic face cream" or "bakuchiol serum."
This Month
01
A/B Test Homepage + Collection page

Skincare Quiz: Design + Logic

Build a guided quiz that matches visitors to the right Froya product based on their skin concerns. 3-4 questions covering skin type, primary concern (dryness, aging, dullness, sensitivity), current routine complexity, and ingredient preferences. Maps answers to specific product recommendations. Froya has multiple SKUs across serums, creams, and oils. The quiz removes decision paralysis and guides first-time organic skincare buyers to the right starting point.
* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.
Estimated Launch
Weeks 1-2 of Month 3
02
A/B Test Quiz results page

Skincare Quiz: Results + Email Capture

Build the results page with a personalized product recommendation, "why this matches your skin" summary using clinical data, and optional email capture for abandoned quiz recovery. Show the recommended product with relevant clinical stats (e.g. "+79% hydration" for dry skin types) and a clear path to the PDP. Email capture collects zero-party data for retargeting with personalized messaging.
* Concept illustration only. Final design will differ.
Estimated Launch
Weeks 2-3 of Month 3
03
Dev Project All PDPs + Collection pages

Structured Data / Schema Markup

Add Product, FAQ, and Review schema to all PDPs and collection pages. AI search tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) pull from structured data to answer queries like "best organic face cream" or "bakuchiol retinol alternative." Froya's clinical trial data is a strong differentiator, but without schema markup, it's invisible to agentic search.
