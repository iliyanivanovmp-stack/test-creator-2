## Meta Ads (Top 3 Best-Performing)

*Note: These are Norse's own Meta ads, not ads we manage. We manage their Google Ads. Analyzed the top 3 best-performing ads out of many active campaigns.*

### Ad #1: Teen Acne (General)
- **Running since:** Oct 24, 2024 (5 months, long-running = strong performer)
- **Copy:** "If your TEEN has ACNE you must get this. Your teen deserves Acne-FREE skin." Lists all alternatives customers tried before Norse.
- **Creative:** Video showing Kill Acne & Redness Ritual products
- **CTA:** "90% clear in 1 week" / "25% off Acne Ritual NOW"
- **Landing page:** /pages/stop-acne (258,929 sessions/mo)
- **Message match issues:**
  - Ad promises "25% off" but landing page says "45% off / ACNE DAY SALE." Different discount, different framing.
  - Ad specifically targets teens, but /pages/stop-acne is a general acne page. There's a dedicated /pages/stop-acne-teens (87K sessions) that would be a better match.

### Ad #2: Muscle & Joint Builder
- **Running since:** Oct 31, 2024
- **Copy:** "Squat without pain with the Muscle & Joint Builder Balm." Lists use cases: performance, joint pain, discomforts, muscles, spinal pains.
- **Creative:** "13 years of knee pain WENT IN 2 DAYS" with product photo
- **CTA:** "Fix knee pain now! Results or money back."
- **Landing page:** /products/muscle-joint-builder (PDP)
- **Issues found on landing page:**
  - **Currency mismatch:** Jar prices shown in USD ($49, $47, $44) but ATC button and subscribe price show EUR (€119.99, €80). Confusing for US visitors.
  - **Media section copy/paste error:** The media quotes on this joint pain product page reference acne. Fox News: "So far, Norse has helped 500,000+ people with Acne." GQ: "Dermatologists started recommending this for Acne." This damages trust for visitors specifically on this page for joint pain.

### Ad #3: Long-Form Storytelling (Teens)
- **Running since:** Nov 2, 2025
- **Copy:** Long-form native ad style. Mom of 3 teens discovers Norse Organics after years of failed treatments. Anti-Big Skincare angle with ingredient education (tamanu oil, calendula, sea buckthorn, beeswax).
- **Creative:** "Sandra (46), Lawyer, Mom" POV image holding Complete Acne Killer System 2.0
- **CTA:** "I wish I Knew About This Method Sooner... Hidden Teen Acne Cure"
- **Landing page:** /pages/stop-acne-teens (87,446 sessions/mo)
- **Message match issue:** The ad's educational angle (ingredients, Big Skincare critique) doesn't carry through to the landing page, which leads with "ACNE DAY SALE - SAVE 45%." Visitors who clicked because of the ingredient story land on a discount-first page.

### Cross-Ad Observations
- All three ads have been running 5+ months, indicating strong ROAS.
- Discount promises vary wildly across ads: 25% (Ad #1), none (Ad #2), none explicit (Ad #3).
- Landing pages all default to aggressive discount/urgency framing regardless of ad angle.
- No dedicated landing pages matching ad-specific narratives (education, ingredient story, teen-specific).

### Norse's Competitive Advantages
1. **Arctic botanical sourcing story** is genuinely unique. No competitor uses this angle.
2. **Balm/beeswax format** stands out in a market of oils, gels, and creams.
3. **Cohesive ritual/system approach** vs individual products customers assemble.
4. **60-day money-back guarantee** is stronger than most competitors.
5. **Dual product lines** (acne + muscle/joint) create cross-sell opportunities.

### Gaps Competitors Exploit
1. **No clinical/dermatologist validation.** Evereden has EWG Verified + dermatologist testing. Bubble has a dermatologist advisory board. bioClarity has "clinically proven" claims. Norse relies entirely on the natural ingredient story and customer reviews.
2. **Price barrier.** Norse is 2-3x more expensive than every competitor's acne system. Competitors offer entry-level products at $14-40.
3. **Teen-specific messaging is underdeveloped.** Blume, Evereden, and Bubble all speak directly to teens with tailored branding. Norse's teen pages are variants of the general acne pages with minor copy changes.
4. **Trustpilot noted** Norse may be soliciting reviews in ways that don't comply with their guidelines.
5. **Illuminate Labs** (independent review site) flagged potential mislabeled ingredients on Norse's site. Credibility risk.

## Cross-Source Analysis

### Theme 1: Discount Chaos Erodes Trust
The site shows at least 6 different discount percentages simultaneously:
- Announcement bar: "SUBSCRIBE & SAVE 40%"
- Homepage CTA: "BUY NOW - SAVE 40%"
- Landing pages: "ACNE DAY SALE 45%"
- PDP one-time: "SAVE 22%"
- PDP subscribe: "SAVE 45% ON YOUR 1ST PURCHASE" (code VIKING10)
- Collection badges: "BUNDLE & SAVE 21%", "BUNDLE & SAVE 22%"

A visitor who sees "SAVE 40%" in the header, then "SAVE 45%" on the landing page, then "SAVE 22%" on the PDP has no idea what the real price is. This breeds confusion and suspicion, especially combined with:
- Countdown timer ("PRICE INCREASE IN: 00:09:55")
- "TODAY ONLY" / "LIMITED STOCK" / "OUR BIGGEST DISCOUNT EVER"
- Permanent scrolling banner about returns

The urgency/scarcity tactics stack so heavily they risk feeling like a scam to skeptical visitors. Reviews confirm skepticism exists: "I thought I had probably fallen for a scam" (Pamela, from landing page). "I was VERY skeptical" appears as a section header. The site acknowledges this objection but doesn't resolve the underlying cause.

### Theme 2: Message Match Gaps Between Ads and Landing Pages
Each of the top 3 Meta ads has a distinct angle:
1. Direct teen targeting with 25% off
2. Joint pain with specific use cases
3. Long-form education about ingredients and Big Skincare

But all three landing pages default to the same template: discount headline + urgency + before/after social proof. The ingredient education from Ad #3 is nowhere on the landing page. The teen-specific messaging from Ad #1 goes to a general acne page. The joint pain product has acne media quotes.

### Theme 3: Currency Inconsistency for a US Audience
94% of traffic is from the US (per device/location data). But the PDP, collection page, and cart all display EUR pricing. The ATC button shows €119.99 on the Muscle & Joint Builder. Jar quantity selectors show USD ($49, $47, $44) on the same page.

### Theme 4: 51% Checkout Abandonment is the Biggest Revenue Leak
The funnel shows strong ATC-to-checkout progression (86%), but half of checkout visitors leave. At 23,714 abandoned checkouts/mo and $82.96 AOV, this represents ~$1.97M in monthly unrealized revenue. Even a 5% improvement in checkout completion would add ~$98K/mo.

Possible causes (hypothesis, not confirmed without heatmaps/session recordings):
- Price shock at checkout (discount expectations set by landing page don't match checkout total)
- Currency confusion (EUR displayed to US visitors)
- Shipping costs or delivery time revealed at checkout
- Lack of trust signals at checkout

### Theme 5: Price-to-Value Perception
Reviews and competitor analysis converge on this. Norse's $99 ritual is 2-3x competitors' teen acne systems. Two negative reviews specifically cite cost vs. amount of product. The site's heavy discounting (22-45% off everywhere) suggests they know price is a barrier, but the execution creates confusion rather than confidence.
